About this project

What is MusicRoom?

MusicRoom is an app that has been designed specifically for musicians/songwriters. It targets a common problem being that a lot of songwriting is carried out in confined areas that don't necessarily scream creativity when the inspiration for songs actually comes from the outside world, making new memories and experiences. The app makes use of vital song writing tools to make a singer/songwriters life and work as straight forward as possible. Songwriting is a very demanding task which at times can get stressful and confusing. We believe that throughout the constant changes and modifications involved in the song writing process, something should stay consistent, and that we believe should be MusicRoom.

Don’t miss out on a possible masterpiece: the perfect musical idea could pop into your head at any point which is why it is so important to have MusicRoom at your fingertips. When MusicRoom has a range of tools that cover most of the song writing process, it is super easy to jot down your ideas as soon as they come into your head.

You never know what could come along: if you have a musical idea that you want to develop on but you just can’t think of the perfect place to use it, just store it in the app. It could be years before you actually get to implement your musical idea but at least with the app you will never forget it and you never know, it could be the difference between a hit or a flop.

How does it work?

The MusicRoom team has spent a lot of time thinking about how MusicRoom should work and we’ve came to the realisation that a lot, if not most of the time, people don’t write a song in one go. Most of the time it is the combination of multiple musical ideas that form a song. To cater to this philosophy we have adapted the app around two main sections:
The Inspiration: the inspiration section is for if you come up with a musical idea that you would like to quickly take down for later use. You can preview each piece of inspiration in the inspiration tab and if you find the right place for it, you can assign it to another idea to make a “songbook”.
The Songbook: the songbook is for the more developed in the song writing process. We define more developed by the amount of musical ideas in the project. E.g. if you have a melody and a lyric then it has developed to more than just a musical idea and is now the basic structure of a song.

Stemming of this songbook/inspiration structure is the actual song writing tools. These are the basic essentials to creating a song: the lyrics, the melody, the camera and the guitar. One of the key things to note about this area of the app is that there are lots of opportunities in which we can, and are planning to expand upon.

Another feature of the app is the feature that reflects exactly what the purpose of the app is, the ability to quickly and seemingly effortlessly record your musical ideas on the go. This is the quick selection as soon as the app opens. The quick selection allows the user to quickly select the tool they want to use to take down their idea and then that idea can either be assigned to a song on the spot or saved for use at a later date. This feature significantly improves the apps practicality and the apps user experience.

Collab

Collab is a feature that is responsible for a lot of excitement amongst the MusicRoom team. Shortly after MusicRoom is released on the iOS platform, this feature will be implemented. The idea behind Collab is that very often, more than one person is used to write a song, whether it is a group of songwriters working together, a songwriter and a musician or even a band. Collab will be a subscription-based social aspect to the app so that users will be able to effectively communicate with each other. There are many situations that Collab would be extremely effective:

If you want to work with another musician/songwriter: Collab will allow you to work closely with another musician songwriter for more ideas when you need them. Collab will allow as many songwriters/musicians as needed and each member can contribute to the song writing process. When a change is made by a member of the group, the other users will be notified and then can start a conversation where they talk about the changes made.

Asking for help or getting an opinion: maybe your song is missing something and you haven’t a clue what, ask for help from other songwriters. This feature also applies to if you are working on a song and you want a second opinion on it. As well as these features, Collab could also be an amazing new way to get in contact with other songwriters and maybe even gather a following.

Meeting your Expectations

The team at MusicRoom is determined to make sure that the app starts at a very good position in terms of user experience and feature quality. In order to do this, the basic UI design has been re-created multiple times and thoroughly thought out in order to make the app as practical as possible but at the same time, visually appealing. As a designer working very closely with a top developer, we have created a very effective balance between form and function.

Our Plan After Funding

The money that we get will be put towards three main areas of the app to get the universal iOS app released:

The design: We have made a lot of progress in the design aspect of the app and we have most of the UI figured out. There are a few future updates that will be significant in terms of the UI design and we want to execute this design perfectly. It is also important to us that the finishing touches have been put to the designs.

The development: the development is the biggest area in which the funding will be used. It is vital to the MusicRoom team that the app is smooth to run and is as efficient as possible. After all, our market should be using this app daily, which won’t happen with a less than satisfactory user experience. As well as the development of the app in its current stance, the funding will also go toward the research of the essential future updates.

The marketing: it would be very careless to put so much effort into the actual creation into the app and didn’t even think about shouting about it. Some of the funding will be used for the marketing of the app, primarily via social media. The MusicRoom team has a very good social media manager who will be in charge of the marketing aspect of the app.

Meet the Team

Phillip harper: My name is Phillip harper and I am the CEO and lead designer of MusicRoom. I am 19 years old and currently studying graphic design in LYIT, Ireland. I have been involved in music all of my life, having picked up clarinet at 8 years old, followed by the saxophone at 12 and the guitar shortly afterwards. 2 years ago I became interested in song writing and it is one of the main reasons that I could even think about creating MusicRoom. My knowledge of music, along with my 4 year experience of graphic design (primarily in the UI design area) with TusNua designs, means that I believe I am very qualified to take control of the app. It also means that I have quite a good understanding of what a songwriter needs and therefore, what to implement into the app in the future.

Risks and challenges

The fact that each member of the MusicRoom team are a family and each qualified in the most important areas of the app creation process, chances of risk have been significantly reduced. Because I am both a musician/songwriter and a graphic designer, I know exactly what I want and the most efficient way of displaying it on screen.

The only Challenges will be in the process of development. The app will definitely be created but as in all projects similar to this, there could be development setbacks. However, the amount of preparation and thought we have put into making sure everything is possible to develop means that there are significantly less chances of risks during development.

While there are apps in the song writing area, there are none that are either very similar or as heavily featured as MusicRoom. Technically, MusicRoom will be the first app of its kind and we are confident that MusicRoom will be far ahead of any coopetition that may arise in the future.

About this project

osa Miniatures and Games is proud to  announce the development  of  a  new line of accessories for remote controlled aircrafts. These new products are for RC aircraft of "The Great War" (World War One) and are offering scale plastic models four basic machine guns with our stretch goals expanding our offer up to eight machine guns used on airplanes during the war .

The purpose of this Kickstarter is to help with the preproduction cost of developing model for RC customers to bring four new designs to market: Aerial Spandau (lMG08), Lewis Type II and Lewis Type III, and the new variants on the Vickers. Our preproduction cost include: design time and prototype printing.

Each Tier of support has a number of points attached. This point system will allow our supporters to convert their support in a customized order. At the conclusion of the Kickstarter, supporters will be sent a Redemption Form which will list all the items available, core items, unlocked items and sponsored items, and the point cost for each item. The points are arranged for common plane scales, but you are not limited to the scales listed:. Our “Patron” Special offers everyone the option to order special order items from any period: we will design, manufacture and ship the item it to you. 

WE will be happy to adjust to any scale (between 1/6 and 1/3) that you need. We will  accommodate your model and produce a products in the correct scale. No longer "it's close enough", we will deliver the correct size for your special plane . 

Unique Offerings by Rosa Miniatures:

Type II Lewis Kit The second most common allied aerially mounted machine gun was the Lewis Type II. We offer this kit with both types of stocks: the common stock and a "gun" stock . The common stock comes with the base models, but the "gun" stock is an upgrade or purchased separately.

Alternate Lewis Gun: This gun is based on an Australian War museum piece . The weapon was manufactured in 1916 and we believed that is a Type I (possibly a type II) with the jacket removed. Often mistaken for a Type III, which were not produced until 1918.

Alternate Lewis with "gun" stock

Type I Lewis Kit: The Type I Lewis gun was used on airplanes from 1914 through 1916, usually as a rear seat gun or in the front of a pusher. This weapon was replaced by the Type II Lewis starting in 1915. We offer this kit with both types of stocks: the common stock and a "gun" stock Type One: Lewis with shrouded barrel, "gun" stock and 97 round drum.

Spandau (lMG 08): In 1915 the Germans designed two air cooled "light" versions of the MG08 machine gun: the Aerial version lMG 08 (lower case L) and the Infantry LMG08 . We are the only manufacture that offers the aerial version in addition to the infantry version. The aerial lMG was used on planes from 1915 until the end of the war, the Infantry LMG was used on airplanes from late 1916 until the end of the war. We offer the gun with or without a gun sight ring as the manufacture provided sight was often removed on aerial mounts.

LMG08's

We offer several variants and options of this iconic WWI machine gun including adding a stock for use as a rear-seat or bomber defense armaments. LMG08 with "gun" stock. . Although most aircraft used belts feed from a fuselage magazine, we also have the drum magazine for the MB08. A must for gunner in the rear seat or for those defending the Kaiser's Siemens-Schuckert Steffen R - Series bombers.

.

Parabellum MG14 / 7.62: This machine gun was used on some early war Germen and Austrian-Hungarian fighters, but was mostly used on bombers and as the rear seat armament. The Parabellum MG14 was built by Deutsche Waffen und Munitionsfabriken and contained an enclosed drum belt-style ammunition feed.

Vickers Variants: The Vickers machine gun is the most common Allied machine gun of the war. These were provided and installed by aircraft manufacturers and there are two variations on this weapon: Jacket with no front vents and a downward lever arm. We do not yet have proper photos yet, but these items are shown in our video and will be available through this Kickstarter.

Gnome 9N 160 hp Monosoupape rotary engine, with dual ignition provision:  This item is being offered on our Kickstarter as a limited special. Our model is light weight and has a hollow core for you to mount motors in (we suggest electric: ABS melts at 240C).  

STRETCH GOALS

At each stretch goal we will unlock a new item, which will become available for redemption with the point you acquire with your pledge of support.  For each $1500 above our original goal we will be conducting a survey (vote) of our supporters to see which item we will Unlock.Unlocked Items will be available as soon as they are drawn (plan on August or September). 

Schwarzlose

This machine gun was used almost exclusively on Austro-Hungarian aircraft and on some aircraft provided to the Turks during WWI. The Schwarzlose featured a delayed blowback mechanism and was a very simple design, but synchronization with propeller was an engineering nightmare in the original M7/12 model as the system could only be operated safely in a narrow range of engine rpm. Austro-Hungarian fighter cockpits had large tachometers. The problem was corrected in the M16 version of the Schwarzlose.

Colt-Browning Mod.1895/1914 AND Marlin M1917 Plus 5% bonus points

The Colt-Browning M1895 was obsolete by the beginning of WWI.In1914 Marlin purchased the dies and tooling for the Model1895 and updated the design into the version 1914.The Mod1895/1914 was also known as the Marlin Gun and was used by the USA, Great Britain, Kingdom of Italy, Russian Empire and Canada. Italy purchased significant quantities this weapon in the 6.5 mm caliber and many ended up on Italian aircraft during the war. In 1917 Marlin developed a second more radical version of the M1895 by using a linear gas piston in place of the "potato digger".

Want to make your Nieuport special? Make it Italian and arm it with Colts!

Hotchkiss M1909 machine gun

Originally adopted by the French army as the Hotchkiss M1909 (or Mle 1909) in 1909,this 12 kg gin was not popular until fitted into aircraft and tanks. It should be noted that this gun was used as a rear seat, the defensive armaments on bombers and it was often used in trainers. Although "obsolete" by 1918, the weapon was used in various capacities through WWII. There are two variants: Enfield manufactured the weapon under the designation "Hotchkiss Mark I" and used a standard British.303 round; In the United States the weapon was adapted to the .30-06 cartridge and was designed the "Benet-Mercie Machine Rifle, Caliber .30 U. S. Model of 1909"and the US variant was made in Springfield Armory and by Colt.

Hotchkiss M1914

This machine gun was used throughout the war on a variety of aircraft, but it was not standard issue. Early in the war, several hundred modified Hotchkiss M1914's were manufactured to fire the 11mm Gras incendiary round. These guns were installed for “balloon hunting” and Zeppelin interception missions. Need to shoot down some Zeppelins install this gun on your unique aircraft

Risks and challenges

We are confident is delivery dates of existing designs for a Champaign under $5000, over this amount we will need to expand our production capacity. For stretch goals there is always issues with new designs and artist completing projects in a timely manner. One of the major reasons for delayed projects is acquiring good plans to base our drawings upon. At this time we have good Schwarzlose drawings and have a led on proper Colt drawings. The Hotchkiss guns are more problematic and may have delays.

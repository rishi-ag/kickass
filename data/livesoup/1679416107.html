About this project

This project will fund the opening of a physical space for New Blood Art bringing our online offering to the 'real world'. In the space we will hold regular shows for emerging artists, keeping our commission rates lower than standard physical galleries, so that artists retain the majority of proceeds from the sale of their artwork.

We have a vision for the space, a place where talented emerging artists can exhibit their work and also propose shows, curate shows, hold talks and workshops etc. Collaborative in spirit, a vibrant space, closer to a studio than a gallery where the range and breadth of all our artists skills can be displayed. We believe it’s essential now that we move our gallery into the physical world in order that we continue to grow and provide a meaningful offering for emerging artists. 

We are passionate about this project’s potential and believe it will become an important space for emerging artists.

It is not easy for emerging artists to gain representation in physical gallery spaces and despite their talent it is often more the result of who they know and a chance lucky break. At New Blood Art we actively and relentlessly visit degree shows, art colleges and art studios, travelling far and wide across the UK (and further afield) to identify the most talented emerging artists, so that they are afforded the opportunity to gain the recognition they deserve. This project is the next step in our development, so that we can offer artists the opportunity to thrive and establish enduring professional careers. The space will support all of the talented emerging artists (and next generation of emerging artists) we represent.

The project came about thanks to the encouragement and support of all our emerging artists.

What's Our Story?

Est. 2004, New Blood Art showcases and sells original art by carefully selected emerging artists. The company was founded with the twin aims of supporting talented artists in the early stages of their careers and making original art accessible and affordable to buyers.

Founded by Fine Art graduate and former art tutor Sarah Ryan who, whilst at art college began thinking about the split that was required in the life of most art students in order to exist financially and by the vast gap between artists in training and those represented by galleries. She realized that, at the time, there was no way of easily connecting customers seeking truly affordable original artwork with the artists who were making it.

Sarah went to Botswana to teach art and when she returned in 1999 found that the world had changed. Everyone was online. The Internet provided the missing piece, making it possible to promote young artists and their artwork at affordable prices. Having been immersed in an entirely different world for so long, she was struck forcefully by the potential of selling art by emerging artists online.

New Blood Art continues to seek out, support and sell the work of outstanding emerging artists, Sarah Ryan personally visits around 100 degree shows a year and has developed a reputation for identifying emerging artists at the start of their careers who go on to do well. As a result New Blood Art has been featured widely across the UK National Press over the last decade.

Artwork can be ordered online and delivered directly to your door (or desk) anywhere in the world. Prices range from £50 to £10,000.

Risks and challenges

How will we attract buyers to our physical gallery?
New Blood Art has been a community of artists, collectors and art patrons since we launched the online gallery in 2004. We already have a strong client base who are interested to see the artwork in person. Currently more of our buyers come from London than any other location, so we will be launching the space in London. This project is in part a response to customer demand as we regularly receive requests from customers who would like to see the artwork in person before investing and who we believe will become regular visitors of the gallery space.

Where will the gallery be?
Finding the right space will be crucial and we have identified and viewed a number of potential locations, having earmarked spaces in SW London and the South Bank (Oxo Tower Wharf).

Are we realistic about the costs involved?
We have carried out research and a space of around 500 sq. ft. in London will cost approx. £2,000/month. Detailed costings vary - as an example an appealing unit available at Oxo Tower Wharf (where we would gain footfall from art lovers walking to and from the Tate Modern) is charged as follows:
Available Unit:
Size: 538 sq. ft.
Monthly rent: £1748.50 (incl VAT and service charge).
Lease Term: 2 years.
Electricity and business rates payable additionally.

Will we be able to continue to cover the costs of running a gallery space once the funds have been exhausted?
Funding from this project will kickstart our launch into a physical space, covering initial moving costs and 6 months rent, by which time we are confident that project will have become self funding.

Do we have the necessary experience to open a gallery space and hold shows?
During the last 10 years, we have curated and held many art shows and exhibited at art fairs. We are aware of what is involved in the consignment of artworks, hanging of work, liaising with artists and collectors etc. Additionally we have discovered that there is a strong correlation between viewings in person and purchases of artwork – further confirming the importance of opening a physical space.

What will we do if we exceed our fundraising goal?
This will be a nice problem to have! If we exceed our goal we will look for a larger and/or more central venue which will enable us to show the work of more emerging and early career artists.

When will you open the gallery?
Our intention is to be open for business by September 2015.

About this project

NavCard is an intimidation-free, intuitive way to find and run applications on a smartphone. We are proud to have designedÂ one of the few devices that will simplify how a phone operates.

Â Intended primarily for the elderly and visually disabled, NavCards allow you to sort through phone commands in the form of a deck of sturdy plastic cards in your hands. Tap your phone on the card and your phone will run the command the card is labeled and programmed with.

FIRST TIER SUPPORTERS:

Hands-on experience with our concept is vital to our success. So, we decided early on: Every single supporter gets at least one actual NavCard. Every. Single. One.Â 

This gives you the option to test the concept inexpensively, you can deliver valuable feedback if you want to share with us (we sincerely hope you do) and we'll still be able to generate the support we need to move forward.

BASIC USER'S NAVCARD SET

This set is intended for use by people who might struggle with their smartphones. It comes programmed with a basic set of commands that facilitate making a smartphone easier to use. Even with the other options we've explored, this was the concept that got us moving.

ADEPT USER'S NAVCARD SET

The adept user's set is intended for the average phone user who is curious about the NavCard concept. We took the basic NavCard concept and programmed more advanced applications on the cards. Despite the fact that you might know where all these applications are on your phone, the concept is still very useful when you want to open an application in a hurry, share the application or just plain feel like you don't want to search for it on your phone.

Planned timeline, during and after Kickstarter

We're tentatively beginning our month-long Kickstarter on 4/21. Depending on how the Kickstarter progresses, we plan on beginning our production with the modified inkjet printer we mentioned as soon as we can be reasonably certain we'll meet our goals - before the Kickstarter even ends, if at all possible. We want to get these orders to you as soon as possible, so we see no need to wait for the Kickstarter to finish before we get to work.

We've already been checking out offices and printing hardware, so we don't expect the process of getting into an office to take that long. We have options here and speed will be emphasized. We also have computers ready to go and some great looking cards modeled up, waiting to be printed.

We plan on shipping orders immediately after they're produced, so orders produced during the Kickstarter should ship out right after the Kickstarter ends. The bulk of our orders will ship starting in July, but once an order is complete it will be shipped - even if that's before the proposed ship date.

FAQ

Q: How does this technology work?

A: NavCards have NFC chips built in, which all newer model android phones support. When a phone sends a signal ("pings" the chip), it responds to the phone with the data stored on the chip. In most cases that's a directory where the application is stored.Â 

Q: Does this work with iPhone?

A: No... not yet. The iPhone 6 and 6 Plus have NFC built in but it's specifically for Apple Pay. There's discussion going on to make NFC on Apple devices open to developers.

Q: I want a NavCard that works with an application you don't have listed! What can I do about this?

A: Let us know what you want to see! We can add reward tiers so if we get enough requests for a specific application we'll offer it to everyone.

Q: What do I have to turn on or install to make a NavCard work with my phone?

A: You don't have to install anything. However, you do need to make sure NFC is turned on. It will be in your wireless menu, near WiFi and Bluetooth if your phone has it installed.

We'll be putting the first portion of the funding we receive into one or several printers specifically designed to print ID's. ID printers can be pretty expensive. We'll be looking for a deal on these printers, whether it be new or used, but they'll need to print in color and be capable of running large batches of cards. We have a couple leads on where to get such printers that fall within our budget (around $3,000 apiece) and will meet our production requirements.

Next, we'll be using funding to support our work with the local community to continue product tests and trials. We'll be looking for any information we can gather, primarily to improve on the functionality of our product and secondarily to lower costs. We'll do this by physically sitting down with retired folks at gatherings we'll set up to program, demonstrate and donate sets of NavCards. Our hope is to generate useful information to improve our concept, but we also want to walk away knowing we improved the lives of the people we worked with in our trials.

Risks and challenges

1. GROWING PAINS: If we greatly exceed our funding goal, we'll need to expand aggressively. We're prepared to do so, right down to the lease on an office/manufacturing facility. This is the best sort of problem to have when starting a business and we really want to have this problem.
2. SOURCING ALL COMPONENTS: We're still looking for a source for some of our storage boxes. Having orders pending will help this. We have options, we're just not sure what to go with yet.
3. OTHER HANDSETS: We want to get this system to work with iPhone, but the iPhone NFC standard isn't open to programming like this. We're going to get in contact with Apple to see what options we have available. Otherwise, NavCard works with every single Android phone we've had the opportunity to test, without fail, so long as it has NFC.
4. CHANGES IN APPLICATIONS: Commonly used applications change. We'll have to update our product line frequently to account for this. NavCards should always work on NFC enabled phones once they're programmed but new applications come out all the time that are worth making a NavCard for. We'll have to evaluate some processes so that you can let us know what we should offer.
5. CUSTOMIZABLE SETS: We'd love to offer a scenario through our website where you can click the checkmark next to the cards you want before adding to cart. We could hugely expand our card library if we did this - and you could get exactly what you need.

Also, custom cards programmed to call people that might have photos included, per an uploading process built into the website? Cards programmed with macros to do more complex tasks like, say, unlock your phone + open (insert app here) with one card scan? PC support? We have lots of options to explore. We'll get there.

About this project

#ASIATOAMERICA: WITHOUT A PLANE

by Nathan Ashker (@nashker)

In 2014,  I took a 6-month expedition across 23,000 miles - from Indonesia through Southeast Asia, China, Russia, Europe, and across the Atlantic Ocean - without ever taking a plane.  I documented my journey through photography and shared it on Instagram using the hashtag #asiatoamerica.  I posted images and stories in real time and amassed 50K+ followers who often said, "It feels like we're traveling with you!"

When my travels came to an end, I was left with images and experiences that I wanted to share in a more personal and permanent way - the journey didn't feel over.   I've assembled the best photos and stories from my trip into a book, and with your support it will be published!  This 200 page, 5x5 photo book captures the same feelings of wanderlust, inspiration and a shared journey that following it digitally imbued. 

The project is nearly complete. The images are laid out. The stories are written. The design is approaching its final stages. Now, I need your support to have the book printed and shipped around the world.  I believe in the power of travel to inspire curiosity, bridge cultural boundaries and shift perspectives.  This book captures those ideas and I hope to put it out into the world.  Please read on to learn more, and find out how you can be a part of bringing it to life.  Thank you!

ABOUT THE BOOK

#AsiaToAmerica: Without a Plane is a book about travel, but it's not your conventional travel book. Most travel books are a memoir, guide book or photo exposé - this book is a bit of all three. It's driven by photography, with over 120 images, while brief captions, country introductions and short essays on topics of travel and culture round out the story.  I profile three photographers I met in Indonesia, Romania and Russia, and have a bonus chapter chronicling my experience volunteering in the Philippines before the land journey began.

Who is #AsiaToAmerica? If your book was personified, how would you describe him / her to others? I was asked this question by my book designer and here was my answer:

"#AsiaToAmerica believes in the value of travel, as it pushes us outside our comfort zones. He sees photography and storytelling as powerful tools for self-exploration, and as a way to inspire others. The unknown doesn’t frighten him and the relationships he builds along the way - though transient - are what he values most. He doesn’t plan much. He relies on local advice, works hard to get off the beaten track and doesn’t mind skipping a popular destination in favor of a local experience. He says yes to new experiences, is spontaneous and follows his gut. #AsiaToAmerica isn’t afraid of flying, but loves a good challenge."

A SHARED JOURNEY

I was fortunate to take an extended journey across a diverse array of cultures. Most people never have this opportunity and I quickly felt like I was traveling not just for myself, but for thousands of people following on social media, many who said they were ‘living vicariously’ through me.  I believe this feeling of a shared journey is what led to my Instagram following growing to over 50K people.  Strangers around the world awaited my posts and kept me motivated when I just wanted to lay in bed all day after a sleepless overnight train ride.

Apps, social media and smartphones made this trip different in many ways from previous ones I’ve taken, but having a digital sphere of people alongside me for six months is a true testament to technology’s ability to inspire and foster genuine connection. This book takes the best aspects of that digital journey and puts it into a form that is permanent and will continue to inspire wanderlust.  In a world of sound bites, dire news reports and transient technology, I’ve assembled a book - yes, a physical book! - that tells an episodic, uplifting story where beautiful images take you to far off places and local insights lead you off the beaten path.

SEE THE BOOK GET PRINTED!

If successfully funded, I will visit the factory in Hong Kong where the book is being printed and document the process. I'll tell the stories of the people who work at the factory and show the book being made. I'm funding this trip on my own dime. It's not every day that you get to see inside a book factory, and I can't wait to share a behind-the-scenes look at the creation of this project.

WHERE DOES THE MONEY GO?

Most of the money will go to having the book printed and shipped to the USA, then distributed to you.  I'll order 1,000 copies from my publisher at a cost of $17,000.  That includes all the services and processes of having the book professionally printed, so you can be assured that you'll receive a high quality product.  I've also accounted for other expenses such as a designer, editor and videographer, as well the Kickstarter fees, credit card fees and costs of producing the rewards.

THE BOOK IS NEARLY COMPLETE

Two talented friends helped me develop, edit and design the book. A literary agent helped me shop the book to publishers and think through the logistics of bringing the project to life. We found a publisher who believes in the story, is impressed by what we've created, and is ready to print the book as a self-published project.

ABOUT ME

I was born and raised in the American South.  In 2004, I moved to New York City to study theatre, then began a career at a technology company (yes, I know, totally unrelated... but that's life!).  I've always been adventurous and  have visited over 30 countries.  I relocated to Hong Kong in 2012 for work and took up photography as a way to share my new life with family and friends back home.  I loved Hong Kong and didn't want to leave, but I decided it was time for a dramatic change in my life.   In 2014, I left my job to pursue a dream of traveling long-term.  My trip began in the Philippines where I volunteered with an organization (All Hands Volunteers) doing earthquake disaster relief.  I then flew to Indonesia and began my journey from Asia to America without taking a plane.  I have about 90K followers on Instagram and see technology as a powerful tool to tell stories and connect people around the world.  I recently settled back in New York City, but my wanderlust and passion for experiencing diverse cultures has me contemplating my next adventure.

Risks and challenges

The back cover, copy edits and proofing must all be finalized. We expect to complete these tasks by the end of June. The production process will take approximately three months to complete. Barring any delays, the book should be printed, shipped and received in the USA by November. My publisher will then ship them to you in time for the holidays.

I plan to be in Hong Kong when the book is printed. I will organize my trip around when it is scheduled to come off the printing press, but if unforeseen issues delay the production schedule, I could miss the process. I really hope this doesn’t happen, as I want to see and document the book as it comes off the press, but it is a possibility.

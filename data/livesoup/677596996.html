About this project

"Incredible paintings of sci-fi suburbia will make you wish you were Swedish"
 – THE VERGE

Simon Stålenhag's retro-Scandinavian sci-fi images have been wildfire across the internet in the last few years. Now - not only is his first artbook coming in English for the first time - with your help, we will publish a brand new second book as well, continuing his amazing story. 

With this Kickstarter we are seeking funds for two lavishly produced art books with homegrown sci-fi from Sweden. These are:

  • Book One: Tales from The Loop
  • Book Two: Swedish Machines, Lonely Places

The first Tales from The Loop book was released on the Swedish Book Fair in September 2014 and was an instant success. The book contained the full first set of paintings from The Loop, the enormous particle accelerator hidden beneath the pastoral Swedish countryside, as well as a score of short stories chronicling life in the shadow of the machines. For the first time the deep and layered setting behind Simon's paintings were available to the public. And soon, thanks to your help, this book will be available to the international English speaking audience.

But with this Kickstarter we are seeking funds for publishing not only one, but two beautifully crafted art books schock full with Simon Stålenhag's paintings and stories. The first book is translated, layouted and ready to be sent to the printers. The second book, with the working title Swedish Machines, Lonely Places is in development, Simon has done roughly half of the new paintings and we are planning for a late 2016 release for this one.

Simon's paintings and stories take place in an alternate version of Sweden in the 80s and 90s. The central location is the countryside of Mälaröarna, a string of islands and half islands just west of Stockholm. The background is this:

In the 1950s, the Swedish government orders the construction of a large particle accelerator. The state agency RIKSENERGI is tasked with developing this massive project. In 1969 the The Facility For Research In High Energy Physics is ready, located deep below the pastoral Mälaröarna-countryside. The local population soon calls it THE LOOP.

From it's inception to it's closure in 1994, The Loop was the largest accelerator in the world. The thousands of staff: scientists, engineers and maintenance workers, all serve Riksenergi during these years – and makes possible tremendous scientific advances. But the power of the Gravitron, the heart of the accelerator, proves difficult to control. The side effects of the project are dramatic. Strange sightings and bizarre rumours taints the scientific image of The Loop.

In the shadow of the weird machines filling the countryside, life continues as normal. The kids of Mälaröarna grew up living above the technological marvel of The Loop, but for them it was just a part of their very ordinary lives. Until strange beasts from another time showed up, that is.

You can see a lot more of Simon's paintings at his Tumbr or website. Go have a look!

Swedish artist Simon Stålenhag burst onto the art scene in 2013 when his first series of paintings were shared on the Internet. His original blend of naturalistic landscape paintings with science fiction elements and a very low key recollection of growing up in the eighties struck a chord. Not just in Sweden, but all over the world. The Verge, Wired, IO9, Scientific American and The Guardian have all praised his work. But until late 2014, the only place you could watch his art was in digital form on the Internet.

Based in Stockholm, Sweden, Simon uploaded his first digital paintings onto the Internet in 2013, since then he has become something of a phenomena in the art and sci-fi communities. Simon shares his time between a small cabin at Mälaröarna (the setting that inspires his work) and an apartment in Stockholm.

FAQ

What about shipping? 

Shipping will be charged through Backerkit. Please be advised that if you order both books, we will charge you for shipping twice as the later book will be shipped in 2016. Shipping is always tricky as rates differ and change over time but we estimate that it will cost 10 USD within the US and the UK, and 15 USD to the rest of the world.

But will not shipping be super expensive as you are located in Sweden?

No! We are working with Shipwire for this Kickstarter and will have fulfilment centers in the US and UK. That means that we offer domestic shipping rates within these countries, and shipping to other countries will be from the closest fulfilment center.

Hey! I just bought the Swedish version and paid a lot for shipping across the world... 

We hear you. Let us know and we will pay for shipping of book one, regardless of which edition you choose.

What is the format of the books? 

The books will have a 280 x 250 mm landscape format.

What is the page count?

128 pages for Book One. It is too early to say what the exact page count will be for Book Two but it will be in the same ball park.

What kind of quality can I expect the books to have? 

Both books will be printed at our printer of choice who are specialized in premium grade art books. This is the same printer that most major publishing houses in Sweden use for top quality art books. Both editions of the books will have hardcovers, clothed spines and be printed on sturdy 170 g semi-matte, fully coated Arctic Volume White paper.

Will the books be available in book stores?

We will most likely team up with an international distributor so that the books will find their way to retail stores in US and UK. However, that is beyond the scope of this Kickstarter. The Limited edition will not be sold through retail stores.

Risks and challenges

When it comes to any Kickstarter project shipping and logistics is a challenge. As we are based in Sweden we have teamed up with Shipwire so that we can use their fulfilment centers in the US and UK.

Another risk is misprints of some sort. To avoid this we will work closely with the printer so that the finished book will be of top quality.

The first book is translated, layouted and ready to go. However, the second book is still in production, so naturally we could potentially face a delay there. However, we have set a deadline were we are comfortable that we should be able to deliver without any problems.

We have previously delivered on two successful crowdfunding campaigns using Indiegogo.

About this project

Marguerite - The Queen of Burundi

She is the Queen of Burundi. Not by profession, but in people’s minds and hearts. She saved more than 30 000 children, orphaned by the Civil War 20 years ago. And not only saved their lives, Marguerite Barankitse, or Maggie, built houses and schools and educated them to be some of Burundi’s most promising young women and men. Today they are found on all levels in the Burundian society.

Maggie, the young, unmarried teacher, who promised that she would dedicate her life to save the lives of others. Her work didn’t stop after the Civil War was over. Now she is close to freeing 1 000 children from prisons in Burundi. In 2008 she opened the country’s most modern hospital. So far they have treated more than 80 000 patients. She has built high standard schools for children in Burundi’s poorest area.

President Pierre Nkurunziza named her “The Mother of the Nation” in a speech two years ago. She is a Nobel Peace Prize candidate. At the same time she is on Burundi’s most infamous list. Sunday, 15th of March, Burundians woke up to the news that five prominent citizens of the country were put on a death list. Marguerite was one of them. Another is the opposition politician Agathon Rwasa. Monday 16th of March his wife was shot while she entered a hairdressing salon in the capital Bujumbura.

“I am not afraid, but at the same time I am not naive,” says Marguerite, who now get help from friends so she can go in a new car every other day. She is not sleeping in her own house. Sometimes she moves around in disguise. Because her enemies are powerful. They belong to the political elites. May be even the President himself. They certainly don’t like her speaking the truth. Saying that Burundi is a rich country where 70 per cent go hungry to bed every night. Not because of lack of resources; Burundi is one of the greenest and most fertile countries in East Africa. Water is in abundance. But the political elite is too preoccupied with their own needs to think of the people. This has made Burundi one of the poorest countries in the World.

The country's current president, Pierre Nkurunziza, announced 25 th of April that he planned to stand for a third term. Opponents say it is unconstitutional and threatens a peace deal that ended the 12-year civil war in 2005. The government has forbidden protests against the president's candidacy, and hundreds of policemen have been deployed. Still, the protests are taking place.



“I refuse to be your enemy” – says Marguerite through the media when she is interviewed. “Those who want to get rid of me suffer more than me. They suffer from paranoia and fear. Fear of losing the positions they are now misusing to enrich themselves. But I’m not seeking revenge. I wish that they dared seeing me face to face. So I could tell them directly that our vocation is to save lives. Not to kill."

” The Civil War in Burundi that started 20 years ago killed more than 300 000 people. It gave war traumas to many more. "But the same war made me stronger. It gave me the strength and courage to manage all I’ve done in life, and I will not give up now,” says Marguerite. “But my only brother is worried. He can’t sleep at night. He thinks that elements inside the regime will manage to kill me before we reach the General Election on June 26th. Then I say to him: Remember our father died when he was only 29. I’m 58 and have had an incredible life. And even if they kill me, they can’t kill the ideas that I’ve been fighting for.” The name Maggie’s grandfather gave her, Barankitse, has proven to be right. Barankitse - the one they are against.

Development of the documentary so far
Award winning director Oystein Rakkenes was contacted by Kristin Eskeland of the Quakers in 2011. She wanted to bring awareness and publicity around the Peace and Reconciliation work that is being done in the rual areas of Burundi. Oystein went to Burundi the first time in 2011. He then was introduced to the incredible life story of Marguerite Barankitse and decided to go back to meet her. He started the Film project with Marguerite in 2013 on two different visits to Burundi. In March this year he spent a month with her in Burundi. Oystein got an exclusive insight into the daily life at Maison Shalom, at the Hospital she opened in 2008, and also the ground breaking project of freeing 1 000 child prisoners. He has made several in-depth interviews with Marguerite as well as meeting key figures in Burundi. People like the minister of Home Affairs and key figuers from the opposition and NGOs. Oystein says: "The death threats Marguerite received were for real. We all lived under great tension and my burudian assistant was tried abducted by people from the Secret Police." 

What we need funding for
The situation in Burundi has drastically changed the last few days. And for Marguerite her security situation has gone from bad to worse. She and other members of the Civil Society could face arrests or assassination attempts any time. We need to go to Burundi to document what is happening, and to film the violations against human rights and the democratic forces of Burundi. The world need to know about what is taking place. The film-team could be an extra support and protection to Marguerite. Because so far the authorities have been afraid of attacking Europeans. Marguerite said to Oystein in March: "You were my guardian angel during your stay in Burundi". 

The film team need money to travel to Burundi now and stay in Burundi until the Presidential election in June is over. We also need funding to edit and complete the film. By supporting us you support Marguerite and our goal is to SAVE MARGUERITE - AND SAVE BURUNDI. If Marguerite dies The Queen and the soul of Burundi dies. People will loose hope for a better future. Another civil war will take Burundi 30 years back. By supporting this film you can bring some light to Marguerite, her children and her life long projects in building a democratic and just Burundi. 

By your support we believe the Documentary: The Queen of Burundi will have premiere at IDFA November 2016.

Thank you!



 

Risks and challenges

The situation in Burundi is now very unstable and three protesters were shot and killed Sunday 26th of April. Marguerite Barankitse and many high profiled people from the Opposition and Civil society are in a desperate situation. The film team has already been filming in Burundi and met Marguerite on several occasions. Still, the film team need more time in Burundi in order to complete the documentary. The crew may face problems to get into Burundi and also do the film work in a unstable situation like this.

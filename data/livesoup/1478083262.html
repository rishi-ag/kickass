What Is Feminist Deck?

Feminist Deck is a bunch of cards I drew with feminists on them. They'll include quotes from the women and information on how to support their work. Here's why you want to help out.

Why Did I Start Creating Feminist Deck?

Feminism has helped my life so much that I want to recognize the efforts of people who fight for gender equality.

A woman I admire pointed out that many of our feminist heroes or long dead and gone. As much respect as I have for her, you won't find Ada Lovelace in this deck. You will find women who are alive, vibrant, brilliant, with impressive ideals and bodies of work.

All participants in the final product will have signed a release form agreeing to appear on these cards, but now I'd like to do more than just use their images. I want to promote them, their work, and repay them for their services to the community.

So, it's become increasingly clear to me that it's hard for women to get paid in this industry, which is why $10000 in the Kickstarter will go towards paying them modeling fees.

There's a lot of them. There's like sixty. And the number keeps trying to get bigger on me.

So, Whatâ€™s the Plan?

What I'd like to do is pay each of the people involved as models for the project, take a small fee for myself, pay for the printing of the cards and other rewards, and then ship the sets to backers and also to the models. Then I'd like to make the cards available to the public.

This way, #feministdeck can do what I always wanted for the project to do: Support feminists in the industry. There is an option for participants to waive their modeling fee, in which case it will be applied to the other models, but for many of these women, you will be representing monetary compensation as well as promoting their work to a larger audience.

Breaking Down the Costs

If we get funded, this is where the money will go:

$10000, which is a $150 "modeling fee" for participants.

This part is very important to me. #FeministDeck is about supporting women in the industry, and paying them for the use of their likenesses is the only way for this project to successfully do this.

Most of the women, though, have so far declined payment. For those participants who have decline being paid, this money will be PUT BACK TOWARDS THE OTHER MODELS. Thanks, Gamergate. =)

$7500 for labor, Kickstart fees, taxes, unforeseen shipping costs, and emergency costs during the project's completion. The cushion is also important. This is my first time running a kickstarter and I may come up against fees I'm not sure about. I've done my best to carefully work out this budget with the help of people much more qualified than I am, but who knows, right? Not Kiva all the time.

This comes out to $22,315. In case there is a larger interest in the cards than I originally anticipated, and I have to do another run of deck and variant, I am adding a cost buffer and asking for $22,500.

Risks and challenges

So, I'm poor. The other parts of that money are for printing and shipping the cards and a very small fee for me to pay my rent for a couple months while I finish up the whole of the project. Currently there's over fifty colored pictures, and there's still pictures that need coloring. I'm working on this alone, so any extra money needs to go to paying for the project and also keeping me housed during that time.

This is my first Kickstarter, but I have people in my corner who have my back and have run successful Kickstarters before. I feel pretty confident.

The Imaginelle Collective of Salt Spring Island is currently creating its third Community Performing Arts Production ~ Imaginelle: Alchemy ~ and needs your support!

Imaginelle is a community celebration of transformation dreamed into existence by dance artist and community visionary, Naomi Jason. Weaving together dancers, musicians, storytellers, visual and movement artists, Imaginelle provides a platform on which community can support each other to bring forth everyone's unique creative gifts.

Why Imaginelle?

The 'imaginal' cell is the scientific name for the cells that are responsible for the caterpillar’s transformation into a butterfly. Inside the cocoon, the ‘imaginal’ cells mysteriously appear. Carrying the blue-print of the butterfly, the imaginals absorb the caterpillar cells and join together to co-create this new creature. The Imaginelle Collective uses this miracle of nature as a metaphor to create new stories about transformation.

Who is the Collective?

We are a core circle of 10 visionary artists dedicated to co-creating inspirational art that facilitates the transformation of our culture, offering new visions of living in harmony with ourselves, each other and our world.

How do we create the show?

Like the imaginal cells, the Imaginelle Collective begins its co-creation with many months in the chrysalis of pre-production. Through this creative visioning and exploration, the outline of the butterfly begins to formulate. 

The Collective then begins the work of expanding to include a wealth of community members, holding the blue-print to unveil the many colours of the butterfly. 

Coordinating the now huge cast of 60-100 performers, the Collective works full time to make this butterfly take flight. Momentum builds and the finer details emerge.

Working together!

The two previous years of Imaginelle involved community members of all ages and walks of life and demonstrated what community is capable of in working together. 

Each performance highlights the interdependence of all life and stimulates meaningful personal reflection in participants and audience members alike. 

The ripple effect continues to grow, as energy and excitement for this project activates more and more people to share their gifts.

              We’re not just talking about a performance any more. 

                      We’re talking about co-operative living! 

                                 Everyone has a part to play!

            We are committed to flourishing, and extend this invitation and enthusiasm to you...

Why the Kickstart?

Naomi and the Imaginelle Collective are now looking for new ways to fund this project and make it a sustainable endeavor. In past years, the Collective has graciously received financial support in the form of community grants and box office revenues. This has covered only our basic costs of mounting the production. 

The vast majority of our time and energy has been shared gratis. Your financial contribution allows us to cover our costs before the show and pay the Collective and production crew a small honorarium for some of the countless hours they will have put into coordinating it.  

Our target of $8500 allows us to forge ahead with focus and with even greater support we can pay the artists properly and be propelled to bring this experience to your community!

Why Water & Fire?

Our 2015 production focuses on the apparent polarities of our world and explores the Alchemy inherent within duality. 

Imaginelle: Alchemy presents a poignant reflection of the powerful beauty of both the destructive and creative forces of water and fire. It also aims to explore the relationship between apparent opposites and to discover the subtle and elusive alchemical balance creating our world. 

It beckons us to look deeply at our relationships with ourselves, each other and our planet ~ to simply love this courageous humanity and to respond with humble reverence to all things. Imaginelle: Alchemy is dedicated to bridging polarities and realizing Alchemical harmony.

What's next?

Already, the potency of Imaginelle has inspired various evolutionary projects on Salt Spring Island.  As the Imaginelle initiative grows, Naomi aims to create a template for community productions in other towns and cities across North America and beyond, to raise awareness and momentum to local transition projects. With this initiative, she intends to travel to other communities to support the creation of local collaborative dance and music productions. 

Risks and challenges

The reality is that every penny of our goal is needed to get this show properly to the stage AND the determined world-changer artists that we are, we will make this happen no matter what! With only 2 months to go, we have a lot of work ahead of us and this truly is a labour of love.

In addition to this campaign, we will apply to the Salt Spring Arts council and have also applied to the Canada Council, and are now in a country-wide competition for limited Arts Council funding of $16,000, which would pay the artists for the actual hours and hours of time they've put into the production. (we won't know if we receive this funding until after the show!)

FAQ

  • What do you need money for?

    Great question! I could ask you the same ;) until we live in a moneyless world, we still need money to do things and simply to exist in the regular world.

    In order for us to really put in the professional touch that this project demands, our total budget is $40 000!!!

    A huge part of this is the countless hours we spend organizing, creating, planning and rehearsing together. This year the professional artists would love to get paid for their work, and rightly so! - this means choreographers, musicians, dancers, costume designers, photographers, body painter, videographer, film-maker, etc... then there is the technical crew. 2 stage managers (front and back of house), lighting tech and designer, sound tech, theatre in-house crew and back-stage hands.

    And so. The $8500 actually only covers: costumes, props and body paint, marketing, the theatre, studio rental for 2 large rehearsals, a small token to the people who do all the nitty gritty production organization (like creating this campaign, a small ($200/person) honorarium for each professional creator, another small token to invited artists and between 8 and 10% goes to kickstarter.

    That's it!

    Yup - with a core of at least 20 people, we hope that the profits at the door will honour some of the work that went in.

    Thanks for asking!

    Here is the actual breakdown:
    $2000 for core creative team ($200 each for countless dedicated hours!)
    $2000 for costumes, props, body-paint
    $1290 theatre rental
    $1000 supporting artists: costume crew, videographer, projection artist, photographer, make-up/hair,

About this project

Presenting USBminiPower – the pocket power supply powered from a standard USB port on your computer.

Why:

The adjustable power supply is an absolutely vital piece of equipment for any electronics engineer or hobbyist. However in the vast majority of cases the needs presented towards a lab power supply are quite relaxed and usually within the low voltage range. With the modern component base the current consumption is usually also very low, and in a number of cases a large and fancy (and of course expensive) lab power supply could just be an overkill purchase for many users. 

What: 

The goal of this project is to create a small and unpretentious USB-powered DC power supply which is cheap, easy to use, and able to cover the needs of an average electronics designer. USBminiPower takes no space on the desk and can help in many cases to run small and low-power electronics. 

Have one, have two, have as many as you need.

How:

If successful, the funding from this campaign will go towards a few major tasks:  

1. Manufacturing and delivery of all rewards to the supporters of the project.  

2. Design of new revisions for better power efficiency, wider range output, and other functionality.  

3. Continuous improvements in the firmware provided the fact that a few different models of microcontrollers can be installed on the board.  

4. Creation of PC software for the USB-controlled variant of the board.  

5. Attempt to raise interest and ensure proper worldwide distribution through some already established and well known website for DIY electronics.  

6. If the campaign results exceed the target by enough margin that will allow extra funding for the design and moulding of a minimalistic two-piece enclosure in the future revisions.  

The project will go live only if successful, so please give your support and share the information in your circle!

Risks and challenges

USBminiPower faces the typical challenges presented before any similar electronic project with availability of parts, cost of manufacturing, and distribution being the major contributing factors.
I am trying hard to negotiate the best possible manufacturing package so the boards can reach the end user and be cheap as possible.
Considering a separate option to have them offered as a DIY kit as well.

FAQ

  • What microcontrollers can be installed on board?

    Currently the board supports a variety of pin-compatible microcontrollers from Microchip: PIC16F570, PIC16F722/723, PIC16F1782/1783/1788, PIC16F25K80/26K80, PIC18F24K50/25K50. Some of these support USB, others don't but come with other functions such as better 12-bit ADC.

    Last updated: Wed, Apr 22 2015 5:47 am EDT
  • What about USB 3.x?

    This revision of the board is designed according to the USB 1.x/2.x specifications, where the port is able to supply up to 500mA current. In a future revision this figure may increase to benefit from the the higher current that a USB3 port can supply.

    Last updated: Wed, Apr 22 2015 5:51 am EDT
  • What will I need in order to be able to modify the firmware myself?

    Only a Pickit3 or ICD3, and MPLabX installed on your computer.

    Last updated: Wed, Apr 22 2015 5:56 am EDT
  • Where can I download the schematic and firmware?

    At the moment - nowhere. Should the project reach its goal, I will release all this information available for download, and will continue with the development of new revisions.

    Last updated: Wed, Apr 22 2015 6:01 am EDT
  • Can USBminiPower cause a damage to my USB port?

    No. The outputs are protected against short and reverse current, the firmware monitors the output current and can disconnect the load when a certain threshold is reached. The input is also protected against reverse connection, and the port itself has additional hardware monitoring to prevent possible faults.

    Last updated: Mon, Apr 27 2015 3:57 am EDT
  • What is the maximum current that USBminiPower can supply?

    Since the current output is not adjustable, it depends on the output voltage. The fixed 3.3V output can supply up to 500mA as an absolute limit. The same stands for the 5V output, which is limited by the port itself. The adjustable output can supply up to 1.25A at 1V, and up to 150mA at 14V. Output ripple is maximum 15mV p-p. Of course all these are limiting values.

    Last updated: Mon, Apr 27 2015 3:59 am EDT
  • What is the estimated market price of USBminiPower?

    The market price is a function of many factors, some of which are demand and quantity. At present the price is unfortunately a bit higher than my own desire, and that is partly due to the fact the project has just started and the rewards here will be the from the first ever produced batch. I am also trying to account for an enclosure in the release version. With the time prices will naturally fall down to an estimated market price of less than £10, but the backers here will be the people who have given life to this project, even at a price a bit higher than that. Anything else will be unrealistic promises, and I do not want to mislead people with such.

    Last updated: Wed, Apr 29 2015 5:03 am EDT

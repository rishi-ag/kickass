About this project

Thank you for considering supporting Departed Soles Brewing Company, opening up soon in Jersey City, New Jersey!

My name is Brian and I'm the owner of Departed Soles Brewing Company (www.departedsoles.com), as well as the brewer, tour guide, bartender, janitor, and any other title you could think of.Â  I greatly appreciate you taking a minute or two to check out the brewery's Kickstarter and for any consideration you give towards helping me out.

What is Departed Soles Brewing Company?

DSBC is Jersey City's FIRST Craft Brewery and only the second functioning commercial brewery and tasting room in Hudson County.Â  We are going to be the first brewery in the State of New Jersey, and really anywhere nearby, that is specializing in making Gluten Free craft beer!Â  DSBC will be making non-gluten-free beer as well, but more on that later!!

Who is Departed Soles Brewing Company?

Departed Soles is a one man operation currently!Â  Well, one man and one four legged friend. My name is Brian, and the Australian Shepherd by my side is Bogan. Don't get me wrong though, we hope to expand and employ locals quickly...

Brewing became a passion of mine after spending time working at a dive bar in Boston, and then living where Craft Beer seemed impossible to find. Â  In 2009, it became a "give a man a fish, or teach a man to fish" scenario and thanks to my older brother, I was home brewing that Christmas.

Several years later I was accepted in to the American Brewers Guild Brewing School's Intensive Brewing Science and Engineering program, with a focus on Breweries in Planning, and graduated in December of 2012.Â 

We've been looking for property to start our brewery ever since then and are glad that that chapter of the book is almost done being written!Â 

You can keep track of the progress we make on Facebook!

You said "we" there, why?

You will often catch me saying "we" when talking about the brewery. It is something that I wouldn't eliminate from my vocabulary or train of thought even if I could.

Departed Soles was originally a basement-born plan between my best friend and I to start our own brew pub.Â  He was a local teacher who also worked at a nearby restaurant and I was regularly brewing beer. In fact, he was also Gluten Intolerant and that was the reason I was experimenting with gluten free recipes.Â  Nobody should have to drink a vodka cranberry during a football game!

He often said that I would never leave my family business to chase our mutual dream, and he was probably right.

Unfortunately, in October of 2010, he was commuting between his two jobs and involved in a car accident.Â  He passed away later that evening with his family by his side.

So, while it is just one half of the original duo pushing forward with the dream, I know I am not doing it alone. I know that he is "up there," inspiring me and guiding me, getting me through the bad days and celebrating with me on the good ones. There isn't a day that goes by that I don't miss him.Â  He pushed me to be a better person, and without him, I would never have ventured down this path and been as good as a brewer, or person, as I am.

Departed Soles Brewing Company is dedicated to his memory.

I would be remiss, however, if I didn't add here that my family is a HUGE part of DSBC. I would also not be the person I am without my parents, and this brewery would not be what it is without them.Â  The love and support, as well as hard work, got me here, and my brothers' work cannot be taken for granted. Thank you all!

Isn't the name a little depressing?

Despite what you may gather from the above story, "Departed Soles" is not meant to be morbid, or about death at all!Â  It is "Soles," not "Souls," after all.

Departed Soles is about the people that do something a little different, defy the odds, go against expectations, chase their own dreams, etc. Departed Soles are not the people that walk in line, but get out and march to the beat of their own drum.

Departed Soles beer is about going against the grain!

Is the beer actually good?

During my final week at the American Brewers Guild Brewing School we hosted a bottle share at the house I was staying in.Â  Amongst the many good beers from around the world that were available, I presented my teacher with GoodbiPA, the flagship gluten free offering of Departed Soles. He liked it so much that he brought it to his wife and presented it to her. She took a sip and said "Mmm that's good, what is it?" His response was "Would you believe that's Gluten Free?"

A few weeks later, the same beer took home a BEST IN SHOW award at New York City's Homebrew Alley, winning its category against glutenous beers along the way.Â  There were approximately 777 entries in this contest, with the category it won being the most entered amongst all BJCP categories.

(The events of that night before finding out I won the awards are a great story... ask when you visit the brewery!)

Since that time, my brewing has been nearly 100% dedicated to perfecting Gluten Free Recipes.Â  We're not just making a gluten free beer to appease a particular crowd - we are making a Gluten Free beer that folks with and without the intolerance will enjoy.Â  We don't want you to have to buy a 6 pack for a friend... we want you to be able to buy a case for everyone!

DSBC will launch with two beers in distribution: GoodbiPA, our flagship Gluten Free IPA that rings in around 6% ABV, as well as A Dark Night, our Black IPA that is also made of 100% Gluten Free ingredients.

Our tasting room, however, we will feature a rotating selection of beer that is both Gluten Free and Non-Gluten Free.Â  Our initial selection will include a Nut Brown Ale, a Red Ale, and more.Â  These beers will be scaled up to the full system once our keg fleet can satisfy the demand.Â  The Tasting Room will also feature collaborations with local brewers and breweries!

***We are currently going through the certification process to call the beer "Gluten Free" officially, but they are and will be made with 100% gluten free ingredients regardless***

Why did you wait so close to opening to launch a Kickstarter?

I launch this campaign knowing that it may look a little odd.Â  There is the chance, after all, that the brewery is open before it ends (if we keep our fingers crossed, keep working our tails off, and pray very hard).Â  There are three reasons for this:

1) Firstly, I did not want to promise anything that I could not deliver. This thing will happen right here in the Powerhouse Arts District of Downtown Jersey City!

2) Secondly, the craft brewing community is extremely close knit.Â  The New Jersey Craft Brewing scene has been fortunate to see a great amount of growth in recent years and, in light of that, a lot of Kickstarter campaigns.Â  Out of respect for the people running those fundraisers, and the awesome people supporting them, I did not want to launch a campaign while another NJ brewery was fundraising.

3) Finally, health.Â  During the build out process I was seriously injured and essentially out of commission for two months.Â  Not only did this cause some serious delays, but it caused some unforeseen expenses both personally and for the brewery.Â  I could not present myself to you during that time, in the condition I was in, and ask for support while I could not be physically working.

What will the money be used for?

The million dollar question!Â  Or 15,000? We could use more...

As mentioned above, there were some costs during our build out phase that were unforeseen that pushed the brewery beyond the start up budget and initial investment. While many of those bills have been paid, or terms struck, there are still bills forthcoming that need to be addressed, and there are still expenses before getting the beer to you.Â  These funds will not only go to those.

More particularly, these funds will be used on:

  • Gluten Free Certification - This process costs several thousands of dollars and involves site visits, product testing, and special processing procedures.
  • A FORK LIFT!Â 
  • Finishing our build out - Duct work, audio presentations, and installing a fantastic draft system.
  • Virtual Tour - So you know as much as we do, if not more!
  • Expanding our keg fleet - We currently have enough kegs to distribute approximately 20-30 bars. We would like to double that.
  • A Keg Washer/Racker - In order to serve you the best beer, whether it is in our tasting room or out around the Tri-State Area, we need to get it to you in the best manner & condition possible. This means quality equipment, and in this case, ensuring that the way it is getting to you is clean, sanitized, and properly filled. The best way to ensure that is to do it in 1 step, not multiple.
  • Permits - Our state requires a permit for everything, from brewing, to selling, to the vehicle transporting the product. These annual fees are due at the beginning of the summer.


THANK YOU AGAIN FOR YOUR TIME AND CONSIDERATION!Â  PLEASE FEEL FREE TO ASK ANY QUESTIONS YOU MAY HAVE!

Sincerely,
Brian & Bogan

Risks and challenges

While there are risks associated with starting most businesses, we are fortunate enough that the finish line is in sight. In fact, the reason we are doing this Kickstarter is because of incidents or issues that came about during that process, making our forecasting, budgeting, and financing a little off. With this funding, we will be able to get back on track financially and open as planned this Spring or Summer. We will also be able to go back to our planned route of buying all new, all American Made equipment, which is very important to us. At this juncture, there is almost no chance that the Brewery does not open in 2015, it is just a matter of providing you with the best quality beer and best experience possible!

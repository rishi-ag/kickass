About this project

Hello humans! It’s Tyler here. I’m an MA Literature student from Texas, and I’m interested in how ancient mythologies influence modern fairy tales. But why? Well, since you asked!

Myths and fairy are especially important to me (and humanity), because they help us step out of our daily lives, also known as chronological time, and in to a mythic time (a concept formed by Eliade). YOUR mythic time may be when you’re reading comics, or watching Once Upon a Time on TV, or re-watching Disney movies religiously, but whatever “myth” you choose to participate in, it helps you connect with your sacred-self.  

Also, the myth takes you to a liminal space, where you can safely acknowledge and “work out” your human anxieties and desires. Why do you think children’s fairy tales seem kinda messed up when read as an adult? (I’m lookin’ at you, Little Red.) It’s because they deal directly with the suppressed parts of human consciousness, and try to form some kind of resolution. They also take us away from the humdrum of our daily lives; they are fantastic, and nothing can transport us to an otherworldly space faster than magic.

So why does this matter? Well, it matters to humans because it helps us understand WHY we like the things we do, and how we participate in the sacred, even if we’re just watching TV. But it particularly matters to my research because I think I’ve come across the moment when a myth “jumps” to a fairy tale, which shows us that regardless of storytelling medium, these stories resonate with humans. I am specifically looking at the Trojan War myth retold by Benoît de Sainte-Maure in the 12th century, and “Troilus and Zellandine,” which is often regarded as the first Sleeping Beauty story. 

Okay, so here is the semi-complicated part...I’m sure you’re thinking, “Well, get to researching then, Tyler!” But alas! Herein lies the problem. Both of the texts I need access to are written in Old French, and many of the manuscripts are not in Texas. (Sad day, yes, I know!) You know where these resources ARE though? In abundance? The Université de Rennes, which also has unlimited library access and professors skilled in French translation. My French is decent, but translating medieval texts is a whole ‘nother ballgame, one that requires scholarly guidance and personal dedication. So this Kickstarter aims to support my tuition for, and stay at, the Université de Rennes over the summer. For one full month, where I will be researching my butt-off every day. Because let’s be honest, France is expensive, and that’s all this broke grad student can afford, even with Kickstarter backing.

The culmination of this research will be my graduate thesis, which has already been approved, and supported by a full committee. My thesis will be publically accessible in spring 2016 when I graduate. And hopefully *crosses fingers* it will be turned into a book one day. (And if that day happens, I will seriously write all of you another thank you haiku, for obvious reasons.) I know creating a Kickstarter for academic purposes isn't done as much as it is for tactile ideas, but I am a firm believer that creating scholarly research on this topic is just as important as creating a re-buildable couch fort or potato salad.

To be transparent with all of you, I’ve included a complete budget below. This whole shebang is gonna cost about $6,000. As I’ve said in my video, I’ve been granted some funding from the university ($2,000) and I have my own savings ($1,500). Sooooo, this Kickstarter will aim to fill the gap between funds and costs.

 **note** This budget has $2,000 listed as my university funding contribution, and not $2,500 (like in my campaign video). One scholarship I was awarded may not be used during the summer, so I had to adjust. 

If you pledge any amount, you get some pretty cool stuff as described on the right-hand side of this page. (I mean, I think wands and crowns are pretty cool, at least.) But as a reminder, regardless of your pledge, if my campaign does not meet the minimum amount of $2,500, the backings do not count, and will be null and void. So let’s try to get this thing spread around as much as we can, even if you personally cannot afford to pledge. Facebook! Twitter! Reddit! Tumblr! All systems a-go!

And seriously, thank you SO MUCH for reading this, let alone backing me! I’m excited for you to join me on this academic journey, and I’m more than grateful to have your beautiful human face as a part of it.

Also! I will be posting all updates of this campaign on Facebook and Twitter, but you can always come back to this page to see how we’re doin’.

Okay, human friends!

Risks and challenges

The risks and challenges of this research will be, first and foremost, the success of this Kickstarter. That will be the determining factor for the depth (and success) of my research. And all other challenges (most to do with translation and adaptation of the myth) will be monitored and assisted by my wonderful thesis committee, which just so happens to be comprised of three kickass lady professors with PhDs.

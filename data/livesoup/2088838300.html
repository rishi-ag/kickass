Story

In the last couple years Superelectric has grown into a favorite hang out spot for art and pinball fans alike. Every 3rd Friday we've opened our doors to the community and shared the magic of pinball. It has been a lot of fun for all of us. However we always have people asking "When is your next event?" and a lot of sad looks from folks when we mention that we are only open once a month.

We've been working hard to change those frowns upside down. Looking around town for the perfect spot to open a ground floor location. Serendipity and patience have paid off and we are happy to announce that we are moving into the space at the corner of Detroit Avenue and West 65th Street right next to the Capitol Theater.Â 

The new location will be open six days a week from noon to 11pm. Everyone will be welcome to come in and hang out but there will be a small fee to play the machines. We will have some food and drinks available and are looking into adding a small kitchen in time. We will continue to host parties and events at our studio space at West 78th Street and look forward to getting back to 3rd Fridays once the build out of the new space is complete.

We are really excited to become a bigger part of Gordon Square and this community. Over the last few years we've had the opportunity to work with a lot of the cool businesses in the square like the Happy Dog, Sweet Moses, Toast, and Blazing Saddles as well as with many of the great artists and musicians in the neighborhood.

The new space is really great but will need a few improvements before we can open up. Here are some of the things we will be putting your Kickstarter contributions to:

  • Building an additional bathroom
  • Installing new lighting
  • Paint for an awesome mural
  • Building materials for an interactive service counter
  • A slurpee machine!
  • Coolers for drinks
  • Pinball Parts
  • Token mechanisms

Your support will help us tackle the buildout and get the parlor open sooner.Â We wouldn't be where we are at without your help and if you still believe in the exciting dreams of three artists please help out now.

Stretch Goals

When we decided to take the leap and start our ground floor location we planned on approaching the build out in phases. Our first phase is getting the games and location ready for play. In our second phase we would make a small kitchen so patrons can enjoy some good food and drinks while playing the games. With your help we can make phase 2 a reality sooner.

We are going to be leasing some of the equipment to make a kitchen happen but need the infrastructure in place to make it work. This means additional plumbing and and electrical costs as well as added permit costs. We have set up a stretch goal of $10,000 to make this happen.

If we reach our stretch goal by the end of out campaign all backers who pledged $20 or more will get a choice of a bumper sticker or sunglasses. Backers who pledged $60 or more will get both the shades and sticker if we reach our stretch goal. We also plan on adding a cool plaque in the space to recognize all of our Kickstarter backers and early supporters when we make our stretch goal.

Risks and challenges

Like many historic buildings in Cleveland, 6500 Detroit has changed a lot over the years. The original architectural drawings are not available and would probably not be very helpful considering all of the remodeling that has been done over the last few decades. Because of all of these modifications it is hard to know what lies beneath the surface. Although our plans are modest these potential hidden obstacles could set back our projected opening and make it difficult for the project to be fully realized.

One great relief in this challenge is that our landlord is the Detroit Shoreway Community Development Organization. DSCDO has great connections with the city and a vested interest in making this space as well as the neighborhood a success. We have spoke at length about the project and they have offered to support us in any way they can in terms of connecting us to the right people to troubleshoot any problems that may arise in the permitting and build out of the space.

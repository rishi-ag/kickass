About this project

One Day prior to our Kickstarter campaign launch, we learned Nepal was hit by a 7.8 magnitude earthquake that struck an area between the capital, Kathmandu, and the city of Pokhara. Our very own, Juliet Thapa-Mendoza, learned her brothers were at Kathmandu during this horrific event, we pray for their safe return and we pray for all the people who lost their lives and those in the struggle for recovery. #NEPAL 

MISSION

We believe in creating G.O. Module (Global Opulence Module) homes for the future; to super exceed the norm of small living. Our team believes it is possible living smart without confinement. We have dedicated two years of research and prototype development, directing attention to building details with a purpose in providing sustainable living, eco-friendly attributes and innovative energy effective methods that reduce carbon footprint around the world. 

THE GOAL

Our goal is to offer a G.O. MODULE to be sold at market. With the monies gained, our mission is to create another G.O. MODULE, fully-equipped with medical supplies and fresh water, to ship immediately for donation to a disaster site in need. Our mission is to offer a product that will last in a world of changing economical climate. We strongly believe in customizing small living to enhance lives around the world. Together, we can help reinstate the meaning of prefab homes as a truly affordable tiny dwelling. 

PROTOTYPE and DETAILS 

Last summer, we brought the G.O. MODULE to life. We worked with a great team and a great scientist by the name of Patrick Flemming, who is a scientist at NASA's dispersed offices. Patrick is and was an adviser in proper insulation and non-corrosion methods involving concepts of reverse acidic charges and insulation applications; this means steel collects internal condensation and corrodes from the inside out. Our applications fix this problem. Each module will be coated with state-of-the-art applications in order to effectively reduce heat conduction and corrosion for years to come. 

The G.O. MODULE will include a Murphy Bed wall unit. H-82 1/2" W-68" Depth 21 3/4" Projection 90 1/4". 

European Wet Room: curb-less shower, stone sink and composite toilet make the space ergonomic by blending the floor spaces together as one. 

The success of the Excel design means a three-chamber unit could be specifically designed for those with no continuous 110 volt supply. The Excel NE, first launched in 1981, has long been the standard toilet for those living off grid. A 1998 redesign gave the unit a rounded look, and recessed the drum handle; the Excel NE contains no fan or heater. Odorless operation is achieved by a 4" vent mounted at the top rear of the unit which functions like a chimney on a wood stove. For proper air circulation, the vent should be installed vertically and 2-3 feet above the peak of the roof.

FUTURE GOALS

Future Goals: In order to effectively budget and make our mission possible after our first Kickstarter campaign, we will donate a G.O. MODULE after every second sale. This will give us room to carefully manufacture and ship each module with the monies we have allocated in regards to our mission. 

We have incorporated a budget surplus that helps our team to repeat our mission. Our goal is to continue a business that creates a budget surplus which enables Charles London Group the ability to donate a G.O. Module that is ready to ship after every second G.O. MODULE  is purchased. 

How Funding Will Help 

With your help and backing, our plan of action is to effectively direct the monies raised toward building two fully-equipped modules. Our objective is to sell one and donate the second to a war-torn country or current disaster site area. The donated G.O. MODULE  will be stocked with basic medical supplies and will be used as a medical unit, then donated to a person or family in need. We plan to repeat this mission with your help. Together we can help restore faith in humanity to areas hit hard by unforeseen global events. 

The monies donated will enable our team to purchase the materials needed for the first G.O. MODULE. We will also utilize the budget for video production and editing time. This budget also contributes towards film time, graphics and editing for our final video showing the building process and donated shipping event.

We invite you to become part of our mission in offering obtainable dwellings for many personal uses as well as our most important use of all, helping those in need during a time of disaster and despair.

Risks and challenges

We have dedicated many hours on the efficacy of the G.O. Module prototype. We have established networks with a World Premium Independent Freight Forwarding Network
(Globalink Network) http://globalinknetwork.com

This network is key in shipping the donated G.O. Module from the United States to disaster sites around the world. The risk that may surface is that once the G.O. Module reaches the destination for donation. Accessibility issues could be present during transportation of the module from the port to the area in need as a result of possible rubble or large debris. Our solution is to be present during this event and help in every way possible.

As for building the module, our group built a prototype in 2013 and worked day and night to ensure proper structural integrity and steel framing methods as well as state-of-the-art insulation that super exceeds standard insulation, offering safe attributes and the final prototype functions exactly how they were designed and imagined. Testing required living in the G.O.Module for 3 months during the hottest Texas heat, as an off grid tiny dwelling, and 1 month during the coldest winter. We are excited to note the Module was a success during hot and cold conditions.

Our challenge was not having the capital to produce another on demand. With funding raised at Kickstarter, we can achieve building another G.O. Module to Market and launch the mission of supplying those less fortunate in the world with many donated G.O. Modules to come.
Together we can get over any challenges.

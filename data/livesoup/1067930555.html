About this project

Radian connects to your camera via the USB or trigger port and is wirelessly programmed from your phone using Bluetooth. Radian makes shooting time lapses simple and intuitive so you can focus on your shot.

Radian can move as slowly as 20,000 discrete positions within 360 degrees. This level of angular resolution is critical for taking smooth motion time lapses and is a important component of Radian's mechanical design. While many devices can move your camera, few can move them this accurately.

Wirelessly control your camera and timelapse settings from our Android and iOS apps built exclusively for Radian. Take advantage of the freedom of bluetooth technology with a 100'+ range, and experience how easy motion time lapse can be.

With the simplicity of USB, you can set your Aperture, Shutter Speed, and ISO preferences all from your smartphone through Radian. Radian will update your camera to adapt to changes in light for a perfect time lapse every time.

The combined power of Bluetooth and USB means you'll be able to receive image thumbnailsÂ on your phone in real time to make sure you are getting the shot you want. If we reach our stretch goal, we'll take this even further with a compiled preview of your time lapse - all in real time!

Radian connects to cameras either through the USB Port or the external trigger port. Canon, Nikon and Sony cameras are able to use all of the features listed on this page because theyÂ plug into Radian via the USB Port.Â 

Radian can also control your camera via the trigger port for other brands (Olympus, Fujifilm, etc) but a few less advanced features.Â To see an exact list please see the FAQ on Camera Compatibility.

With a single Radian, you can create beautiful motion time lapses whether you are shooting with an iPhone or a 5D Mark iii.Â 

Grow With Radian and add more axes as your ambition grows. Start with a single Radian for panning motion, and add an L-bracket for vertical tilting, or a linear adaptor kit for sliding.Â 

Program up to 3 Radians at once to effortlessly drive complex multi-axis motion shots. During your time lapse, the Radians will move in synchronization so that your camera is perfectly still while taking photos.Â 

The Radian linear adaptor allows Radian (version 1 or 2) to motorize a linear dolly system.Â The linear adaptor kitÂ is designed to mount your Radian quickly and securely to your favorite slider. With all-metal components, and weighing only 12 ounces, this adaptor kit wonâ€™t be slowing you down.Â 

We are building adaptor kits for two of our favorite slider systems (StudioFX and Rhino Slider), but the mounting hardware itself is designed to be easily adapted for our DIYers as well. We love to see your home-built rigs!

Radian can also be programmed using Keyframes, an easy way to program non-constant motion.Â 

With Radian's USB control of your camera, you can effortlessly take day to night time lapses without having to touch your camera. One of the big issues with taking Holy Grail timelapses is that you often will change your settings manually on your camera. This means while touching your camera you are exposing your timelapse to camera shake, which is especially an issue with motion timelapse. With Radian, you can either adjust your ISO or shutter wirelessly from your phone, or program the Radian the ramp ISO and shutter for you.

The original Radian was designed to make stunning motion time lapse accessible to photographers who didn't want to have to build their own motion systems. We built a precision motion device with extremely fine angular resolution (See Tech Specs above!) that also had an intuitive smart phone app. The combination of accuracy and ease made Radian popular among amateur photographers and pros alike. We are equally proud to say that Radian has been used by pros like BBC Earth and National Geographic and by hundreds of people just getting into photography.

Radian can be used as a move-shoot-move device for creating time lapse videos, or it can be controlled to create Panning or Tilting Video at speeds of up to 4.4 degrees/second.

  • How is Radian 2 Different from Radian 1?

    Radian 1 was built on Kickstarter and gives photographers the ability to shoot panning and tilting timelapses. Radian 1 is programmed from your smartphoneâ€™s audio jack and requires the user to plug their phone in during the uploading process.

    With Radian 2, your phone is now connected completely wirelessly over Bluetooth, which makes programming easier and creates ability to easily adjust of your time lapse settings mid-shoot without having to disturb your camera. Radian 2 also now controls your camera via the USB port meaning that you are able to control all of your cameraâ€™s advanced settings (Shutter Speed, Aperture, ISO) wirelessly from your iOS or Android device. USB control also allows for advanced Shutter and ISO Bulb Ramping, as well as the ability to move thumbnail images from your camera to your phone, so that you can track the progress of your time lapse as it runs.

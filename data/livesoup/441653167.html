About this project

The Film: The Isolationists and the Program Module

“The Sunset Tapes” tells the story of three men, David, Thomas, and Will, whose lives fall into disarray as they desperately seek gratification and fulfillment in the broken, confused technology driven landscape of 21st century existence.

David, an illustrator, is obsessed with breaking out of his daily 9-to-5 grind and sees the opportunity to do so with Lindsay. Thomas sees himself as being above his situation and will go to great lengths to prove it. His opportunity arises when he meets Madison, a young woman who is in a precarious situation and needs his help. Will is a person fixated on his baser impulses, who disregards his wife and goes to great lengths to be with his much younger mistress.

These men act out their desires and are unknowingly directed in accordance to a script lain out by an ever-present figure lingering always at the darkened edges of the narrative. But who is this figure? Acting in the capacities of performance artist, terrorist, and demiurge, this figure uses the tapes: like fetish items of archaic technological refuse, to reorder space and time into his schematic.

Follow the movie on Facebook!

The film is co-directed, co-produced, and co-written by Brandon Salaz and Salvatore Arnoldo. The two first met as children nearly fourteen years ago, and have been in a non-stop creative collaboration ever since. Brandon brings energy, creativity and a passion for cinematic excellence; ensuring the camera, lighting, and audio all come together to capture the scene being emoted by the artists. Salvatore brings a passion for storytelling, the arts, and literature to weave layers into every word, breathing life into each scene.

As teenagers, Brandon and Salvatore worked together to produce a thesis film for a high school film class. The film was to be a 45 minute surrealist piece called "Delta-Black", inspired by Salvatore's burgeoning interests in filmmakers such as David Lynch and David Cronenberg, and his chief literary influence William Burroughs. The film itself was not completed but the idea of a surrealistic thriller would over the years go through a number of conceptual permutations and evolutions to become what is now “The Sunset Tapes”.

Since their initial attempt, Brandon has received a Bachelor’s degree in Filmmaking from The Art Institute of Phoenix and Salvatore has become a published writer. They have decided to once again come together for what is now a feature-length film. The film represents the culmination of a friendship and creative collaboration which has grown for over a decade, and will continue to do so into this project and beyond. The act of creation and expression through their respective mediums is not merely just a desire, but a mutual need: to draw the audience in as an active participant and thereby touch hearts and minds.

 Celebrating not only the film but the art that makes the film.

Creating a complex film like The Sunset Tapes is by no means a two-man task however. We have had the incredible chance to meet and work with incredibly passionate people who are ready to breathe life into the film. This indeed is an independent film and with an independent piece there is always clear risk, which is why we have waited until now to take that risk and create this incredible project with not only friends but a group of artists who are climbing this mountain with us. 

Which is exactly why this is more then celebrating the film, it is celebrating every single person who works on this project and promoting what they do outside of the project as well. Our team isn't just names in the credits; they are musicians, painters, actors, designers, creators, thinkers, and writers that deserve wide exposure. 

A huge thank you to our team, none of this would be possible without them. 

Here is our incredible cast, we are very lucky to be working with such a diverse and talented group of performers.

Alyssa Eshelman - Production Manager

Alyssa, growing up in Idaho developed a passion for storytelling in the visual mediums. In high school she directed full stage productions and went on to pursue a career of film production where she excelled not only in her directorial skills but in her management and post production work as well. Now a proud graduate of The Art Institute of Phoenix and employee of Arizona Virtual Studios, Alyssa raises the bar higher everyday.

Gabriela Estril - Public Relations, Social Media Manager, Sound Mixer

Gabriela, another valley local, has constantly pushed herself further then her peers. Finding ways to improve and excel as not only a filmmaker but as a human being, she always steps up to the plate. Another graduate from The Art Institute of Phoenix, she found her forte in sound mixing and sound design and has worked on numerous projects making sure the soundtrack is up to par if not above the rest of the project. Now working at AOP as a marketing videographer, Gabriela continues to find new ways to give her the edge as a filmmaker.

Amanda Orr - Graphic Designer

Amanda resides in Mesa. She boasts a large career of graphic designing working for numerous companies constantly adapting and developing new techniques to ensure she can provide the highest quality work. Included in this large variety of graphic design work she also supports her husband, David Orr, as Art Director to his photography company. Together these two make for an electrifying team who not only always bring their A-game to every challenge but are not afraid to find a fun alternative solution.

Alexander Sedig - Illustrator, Story Board Artist:

Alexander Sedig is twenty-two years old and a Phoenix native, he has been drawing for many years, refining his craft into its current deft and expert form. As an artist he is deeply reliant on technology, doing the majority of his illustrations digitally on a Wacom tablet: like the artist's sketch-pad of the twenty-first century, carried always with him in a messenger back slung over his shoulder. He has been deeply influenced artistically by the work of Francis Bacon, and this influence more than shows in his oftentimes abstract and surrealistic portraits, but his style remains all his own. He is featured in the RAW Artist Showcase last September and currently uploads his work to alexandersedig on Instagram. 

Samuel Collett - Sound Artist, Music Composer:  

Samuel Collett is twenty-two years old and a resident of Mesa, Arizona. He could only be adequately described as a musician who operates without boundaries, taking influence and inspiration from everything from other musicians to literature and film. His style is a blend of electronic and experimental music; using sampling techniques to stimulate everything from hyper-kinetic danceable romps to ambient moody tracks. He has been posting on Soundcloud for the past four years under the online handle of Tafty, gradually building a very eclectic and intimidating body of work.

Eric Cloutier - Painter, Camera Operator:  

Eric Cloutier is twenty-three years old, originally from Newton, Massachusetts, he is a longtime resident of Phoenix, Arizona. In addition to being currently enrolled in the film program at the Art Institute of Phoenix, he pursues a wide range of other creative endeavors, which for many years has included painting. His paintings are mostly abstract-expressionist, employing a wide range of mixed-media; partly in an attempt to describe philosophical precepts through raw emotional and psychic expression. His art and philosophical outlook served as a natural pairing to our movie: given his partiality to expressing the purposes and aims of noise music through his pieces, and painting on anything from scraps of cardboard to canvas, decorating the pieces with cigarette butts and dried leaves. He was featured last September in the RAW Art Showcase in downtown Phoenix, and sells his paintings through Paintopia777 on Etsy.

With "The Sunset Tapes" halfway through Pre-Production, we are proud to say that thanks to these incredible individuals our production has been soaring. "The Sunset Tapes" journey began January 1st of 2015 and it has become a mission to #BecomeVirus ever since. 

The script is a satisfying 100 pages long and contains just about 375 shots and features a little over 35 different characters. 

The script has been completely broken down and budgeted item by item.

All the shots have been planned and scheduled with about 100 of them already story boarded.

And 28 out of 35 of our characters have been cast.

Through an Earth Month campaign for recycling Technotrash that is going on till May 6th, we have been able to collect a large number of props and set decorations that will be in the film. Such as a collection of 50 complete computer sets and large quantities of VHS tapes/cassette tapes. 

Our LLC has been obtained and we are even being sponsored by The Changing Hands Book Store in downtown Phoenix.

The film thrives on the art and that is why it is so important to us that we support and acknowledge the incredible community of artist that create in Phoenix. A quarter of the film solely takes place in highlighting not only the Phoenix night life, but our art district as well where we will see our characters take part in First Friday.

Even With all of our best efforts, none of this could be possible without your support. To interact directly with the audience and to extend our appreciation to them for making our endeavor possible with a very personal sincerity that may not be as possible on a major production. This also presented us with the opportunity to promote local artists and businesses on a national, and even international, stage: giving these incredibly talented individuals and colorful, diverse venues just as much exposure as is traditionally allocated solely to directors and actors. And not only this, but it gives us and our team room to be as creative and original as the audience will allow us, without being subject to stipulations outlined by studios or investment groups. 

This gives us the opportunity to breathe new life into the mediums we so love, by shucking away with the tired old tropes and narrative devices, seen time and time again, and breaking new ground with fresh and experimental ideas. And most importantly our success feeds the success of many other would-be creators, by setting an example that others can easily follow: as in this age, with the technology that exists, it is possible to create or do almost anything and distribute it absolutely for free on the internet, no matter who you are or what your background. 

In a word, our goal is freedom: for the individual, the artist, and the audience, which in reality should flow seamlessly into one another.

Risks and challenges

The risks associated with a project of this size and scope are clearly quite imposing and the proverbial stakes are very high. There is the issue of operating on such a small budget, working with a very talented cast and crew only being able to provide minimal pay as incentive and the uncertain possibility of some deferred payment in the future. And then in all of the moving parts and complexities inherently existent in a movie of this size, there is a lot of room for things to go wrong.

The only real safe-guards against the sizable margin for error in these areas is to assemble an air-tight team of talented professionals, driven first and foremost by their passion and a love for their respective crafts and the project: this is not limited only to the key areas which receive the largest media spotlight, such as directors or actors, but also down to the most minute levels of detail. To this end the team is more than equipped in raw commitment and drive.

And spearheading the project are a writer and director who have plenty of experience, through trial-and-error, through success and failure, to accomplish the entire anticipated one-hundred minute run-time and deliver the best possible product. Brandon Salaz, for example, has participated in many, many artistic and commercial projects, working on many student films during his time at the Art Institute and cultivating an accomplished career as a videographer thereafter. And Salvatore Arnoldo has spent nearly his entire life focused on the refinement of his craft, by adhering to a rigorous writing routine and the highest levels of his aesthetic capabilities; studying literature and film as both an education and for a lifelong love of storytelling. In their still relatively young lives they have poured out blood, sweat, and tears for the sake of their mediums and for the fulfillment of their highest artistic goals. This devotion and passion will be at the center of the engine of this film, and it will be clear by the end of this great experiment in independent filmmaking that anything can be accomplished through sheer force of will and the plain and simple drive for expression.

To say the least the risks are quite clear, not only to the creators, but to every single collaborator on this team. This is a micro-budget, this is hours upon hours of work, but it is also a once in a life time opportunity to prove that there is still art and talent not only in the film industry, but in Phoenix: where an incredible population of artists are still growing and to be quite honest, need to be heard. So we ask of you, to not only take this risk with us, but to follow our example by developing an idea of your own and with others Become Virus in your own life.

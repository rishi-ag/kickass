About this project

NEUROPHREAK is a short film we're making for Maine's lauded Damnationland film series. It is being produced and directed by myself, George Dalphin, and was co-written by me and my long-time collaborator Joseph Foster, who will also be voicing the AI "Orange".

"Neurophreak" takes place in a unique but all-too-plausible future, in which everyone is directly connected to the AI that organizes society via chips in their brains, and each person gets social/information input directly overlaid into their vision. Within this setting, we follow a girl who meets a guy who has learned how to meditate up through the system, and take over other people's bodies with his mind.

This is at once the story of an entire society, and a story that takes place entirely within one woman's psyche, and at its core are some of the most fundamental existential concerns, for both the individual and humanity at large. Insomuch, there are a variety of complex challenges involved in depicting the world of Neurophreak, which while complex also provide for the opportunity to experiment with some exciting, phantasmagoric and fascinating imagery. Many of these sci-fi concepts writ-to cinematic-experiments our talented director of photography Mark Hensley and I have already tried, and excitingly succeeded at in concept-proof form.

As part of the Damnationland film series, this movie is already guaranteed a premiere at the State Theater in Portland, Maine, and a tour around New England theaters, as well as being available to tour other film festivals thereafter, which will be the plan.

There are a few large elements of the story which will require complicated and somewhat expensive production procedures, such as for one scene shutting down part of a Portland city street, and coordinating and feeding my largest ever cast and crew, creating certain practical special effects such as for one character who has old, embedded military cyberattachments, a soundtrack including original music by Shriekback written for this movie, and the expenses involved for all of this brings this production out of the realm of my being able to fund it myself.

So in order to aid the process of funding this, among the more standard fundraiser rewards such as the gorgeous Neurophreak poster and Damnationland DVDs and such, I'm offering for sale several of my favorite of my own pieces of artwork, which until now I've kept in my personal collection, and which outside of this fundraiser may never again be for sale. I'm also offering to produce new original pieces of artwork, which I hope you'll buy, because I look forward to creating them.

 So whether you want to buy an heirloom piece of original artwork, an awesome sci-fi movie poster, or just pre-order a Damnationland 2015 DVD with the knowledge that you helped one of the movies get made, we are very grateful for your help, and we can't wait to show you NEUROPHREAK.

Thanks!

Risks and challenges

This project faces all the normal risks any filmmaking endeavor faces, however I can honestly say I am at the point in my experience where I feel excitedly confident, particularly after running the tests we already have run, that we can do all of the ambitious and experimental things we are going to try to do for this movie. Everything is, more or less, already lined up, as one of the biggest lessons I've learned through gradually becoming a real-type filmmaker is that pre-production is the place for most of the real work. And we are ready to do this. All we need are the funds to put the already-placed wheels into motion.

As an example of my capability to complete projects of this nature, however, I would point you to the many finished projects available through my personal website, http://manlikemachines.wix.com/movies

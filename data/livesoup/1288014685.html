About this project

 Have you ever considered the fact that, to be a dog-walker, a business person, a landscaper, an auto detailer or dealer, or a whole lot of things you must have a license...

but to be a parent—you must just---BE? 

How and why is that even reasonable? Human beings possess incredible potential, and require much nurturing and cultivation for a very extended time versus other mammals and animals. Yet, some of us just decide, while others of us don’t decide but rather discover, that we are going to be parents. Then what? How does one go from bringing a little one into this increasingly chaotic, sometimes unforgiving, and often misdirected world, to leading them to higher ground...towards fulfillment of their God-given potential? 

Again, we recognize that for everything from our new I-Phone 6 to our Prius or even our latest smart TV or laptop, we are provided with extensive supporting documentation, on-line resources, and access to on-going and evolving information, forums, and updates. Conversely, with our children, we only receive a few directives as we are discharged from the hospital with a minuscule human being in tow; namely, “position the car seat in the rear of the vehicle with the infant facing the back (infant properly buckled into the seat, and seat buckled into car), put infant to sleep on its’ back, feed (on-demand, then every 2-3 hours), and change diapers frequently as needed!” That is basically it until you: 1) Have your follow-up visit with the Pediatrician or Family Practitioner +/- 2) Have a grandparent or a family member or other friend come around with their version(s) of factual counsel and well-meant (if not often misdirected) guidance, or 3) go to random blogs on-line (some of which are great, others—not so much).

So—herein lies the basis of a need for “Blueprints for Success: Guidance for Intentional Parenting”. This manual is not for wimps---no, it is for parents whom intend to nurture their children and send them out into the world fully equipped, to the best of both the parent(s) and child’s abilities. (Note: If you are a person who desires to have your grown 45 year old son still sleeping on your living room sofa-- rent and responsibility free, while being your best/only companion...unfortunately despite my desire both for publishing success and to be blessed to bless others by sharing potentially positive influence, I will have to at this point recommend that you skip out on this information. There really is no need for you to read any further). If, on the other hand, you watch in horror what is occurring here in the US (and actually in many other parts of the world) in terms of the evidence of the decreased personal responsibility and decreased general awareness and knowledge possessed by chronological adults (people 21+ years of age)—then you really need to continue to follow this story. 

The next important step I am calling you to, following up on getting you to think about the consequences of parental actions and inactions, is recognizing the importance of your immediate valuable actions and contributions towards this Kickstarter project: 1) Financially contributing to my effort to bring this book to completion and distribution and,  2) Promotionally, by sharing with your “tribe” the information about the book, and thus the opportunity to assist in this crowd-funding effort. 

Realize, this process is ALL or NONE. Either we reach the goal, or nothing comes for the funding effort. Now...you may ask, what makes me the person capable of writing this tome on parenting? Why should you give me the time of day, lend an ear in my direction, or trust my insights? Well, I am SO GLAD you asked!

WHO AM I, AND WHY SHOULD YOU EVEN LISTEN TO ME OR SUPPORT THIS EFFORT??? My name is Dr. Yvonne W. Fry Johnson. I was born in Los Angeles, California where I grew up one of two children born to a rocket scientist father (literally) and a mathematician/chemist/physics expert mom. My father was the first in his family to go to college, and did so all of the way to a Ph.D. in Chemical Physics from the University of Southern California (USC) in the 1960’s. My mom received a BS in Math from Lincoln University in Missouri, and a MS in Physics from Howard University in the early 1950’s. I was blessed to attend and graduate from Beverly Hills High School (90210), and continued on to be educated and trained at several places pretty well known around the country and even the world—including but not limited to Stanford University, Yale University School of Medicine / Yale School of Epidemiology & Public Health, the National Institutes of Health (NIH) and the Centers for Disease Control (CDC), just to mention a few. I also trained as a Pediatrician, having completed my Residency in1987 at Emory University School of Medicine in Atlanta, Georgia. I served as an Epidemic Intelligence Service Officer at the CDC 1987-1989, and soon thereafter was a Community Health Center Medical Director. I have, with another physician (Dr CS), personally trained over 1,000 medical directors of CHC's nationally while serving as a consultant with the National Association of Community Health Centers.

Much more significant is the fact that I, with the help of my husband of the past 10-1/2 years, my ex-husband & his wife, and extended friends and church family, successfully raised three unique, self-assured, independent and reasonably successful young people. I honestly am not at all bragging on my children or myself, but rather truly recognize and appreciate that the blueprints followed especially by my mother (with help from my father) in raising my brother and I have been re-visited in the process of raising my own children. Now that I have two children in their 20’s, and the 19 year old is completing her freshman year in college, I see again that what was modeled after these blueprints, was received and is being replayed/paid forward in various ways. 

I see the same in other young folks both in my own circles and in others’ and even much broader circles, recognizing some of those whom I have become aware of are pursuing stellar, unique, rewarding ventures and experiencing successful outcomes. I am joyfully sharing their inspirational stories, and their relevant "blueprints for success" in this book as well. 

I personally am so blessed to see my children following their dreams: spreading their wings, flying away from the nest, and checking back in and letting me know through word and deed that our parental words and actions were not ever for naught.

"Proof in the Pudding"

Our oldest daughter served as the JROTC Battalion Commander during her senior year in high school.

She then majored in animal sciences at Texas A & M University, graduating in 2010. While in Aggieland, she barrel raced on her own horse (Dancer) 

as a member of the Rodeo Club, learned how to play Polo (horse type), and rode her motorcycle. She was accepted into, participated in, and became an alumnus of the 26 week Kentucky Equine Management Internship (KEMI) program--working on a 400+ acre horse farm in Paris, KY. After graduation, she worked in a Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU) at University of Pennsylvania’s Large Animal Hospital, as part of her own well designed and executed action plan to prepare herself to move around and up in the horse racing industry. 

She followed her dream to become a professional racehorse jockey, becoming an Apprentice Jockey (“Bug Boy”) in the fall of 2013.

Having started playing chess at the age of 5 years, and being trained by a Russian Grandmaster, Louis Fry V played chess around the country.  He was one of the top Scholastic players in GA for a season.

In 2006, at 15 years of age, our son was a part of a 25/26 member young male American Scholastic Chess contingency playing chess in Moscow, as delegates traveling with Grand Master Susan Polgar to compete in “Chess for Peace”.

He also interned at the Georgia Aquarium in 2008 & became scuba certified in 2009. 

He has since graduated from the University of Hawaii, Manoa (2014), and is pursuing his ultimate career goal of becoming an attorney.    

Our youngest child, a daughter, is in college at Bucknell University. She selected this college somewhere around 7th – 8th grade, and only applied there-despite its extremely competitive stature. She also switched from playing the saxophone to the oboe in 7th grade, to position herself as more of a standout. Her interests have carried her to Europe as a People to People Student Ambassador; to a role with her high school yearbook as both a photographer and editor; 

and to building on her extreme love of animals by becoming an animal behavior major in college, 

while also participating in training for eventual participation in the Bucknell Equestrian Team. 

So, as you can see, something impacted these children and let them know that they did not have to be exactly like anyone else—in their own family, or in their school or community. They are free to be &/do themselves...whatever that is deemed to be. They are equipped to do so because their parents had some blueprints to follow. When you use blueprints to build a building, it is to better ensure the structure is correct, the foundation is firm, and the cornerstone is precisely set. The same can be said for using blueprints for success in raising children.

WHAT’S IN IT FOR ME (WIIFM)?? (YOU??) So in meditating about how to present this useful information to a broader audience, I was led to try to help meet this unmet need by demonstrating more than one generation of effort towards raising successful children. Through use of anecdotal stories, pictures, and factual information and statistics, I am presenting very clear blueprints for success that can be replicated regardless of your own educational background, financial standing, marital status, number of children, or prior parenting experiences (both how you yourself were parented or how you parented any older children). As the guidebook starts, for each concept presented (deemed an ‘opportunity’), I describe what was done for me in my life (blueprints used/developed), its’ impact on my life, and how it translated into benefits/outcomes. I then follow through with my version of similar actions (evolving blueprints) with our children, and how that has thus far played out in their lives. This is not a biographical discourse on my children or on myself: there are many, many people as, or far more “successful” or “accomplished” by the world’s standards than anyone within my family circles. With that in mind, I am even offering a reward level wherein folks may provide shout-outs to their parents whom have “Intentionally Parented” them. In addition, I am including case reports on other children who demonstrate evidence of such intentional parenting and their resultant success. Using this approach, this book provides a well explained and thus easily visualized road mapping of a series of decisions made with specific endpoints in mind, with demonstration of how those blueprints have subsequently imprinted multiple individuals’ lives, and how you may do the same within your family structure. Included with this information, whenever and wherever available and applicable, are suggested resources and unique and exciting opportunities...some free and others paid. All of this shared knowledge and experience ultimately is provided to spur you and your friends and family members towards creative, thoughtful and focused ways to diligently persevere towards the successful cultivation of some great adults. Our society is so in need of our collective intentional parenting efforts. We often state that, “It takes a village to raise a child”, but we neglect to provide that village with good blueprints! Our role in our children’s lives is to, “Raise them up in the way that they should go”. We are also to, “Leave our children’s children an inheritance”. I do not believe that either of these scriptures is as concrete, narrow, nor as simplistic as often perceived. Rather, our long-term impact on those little bundles of inexplicable joy should be to leave both they and their offspring better off for having had us as willing, diligent, prepared, self-sacrificing participants imparting into their lives. Our personal responsibility is to do our level hearted best to give them the best, so that they too become the best and pass on the best to future generations in terms of self-motivation, compassion, accountability, responsibility, character, integrity, honor, knowledge, skills, understanding, critical thinking, decision-making and humanity.

With all of that in mind, I thank you sincerely in advance for assisting me in my quest to help widely spread information supportive of intentional parenting: that which is unfortunately not so readily available in this day and age, nation and world. So much of the public’s attention is focused on the non-edifying actions of persons in entertainment, sports, and other media (You Tube, Facebook, Twitter, etc.), instead of focusing on doing the right thing, the best thing, the very hard, energy and time-consuming thing—intentionally parenting their young offspring/children. Looking at reality TV is in: dealing with the reality of raising offspring...not so much. Having said all of this, I still opt to believe that if parents know to do better (having blueprints for success), they can chose to do better (adopting or adapting these blueprints for success). But, if parents are ill equipped, they are in no position to well equip their children. Thus, the goal of this effort and publication is to empower more of the population with this not so common awareness and knowledge...sharing of the blueprints! 

We cannot continue to criticize and judge folks for their lack of success in life without offering the tools to facilitate skills towards better lives.I neither feel nor desire that only those who can afford to pay to purchase the information should be privy to this essential knowledge, therefore whenever possible please purchase a copy for yourself and one to share. Thank you again, in advance. Blessings over your lives and those of your children, and may what you make happen for others be returned to you in abundance!

Respectfully Submitted, 

 Yvonne W. Fry Johnson, MD MSCR

Risks and challenges

I have developed a schedule to finish "Blueprints for Success: Guidance for Intentional Parenting" to be ready to print for the release in early December 2015. Only an unforeseen natural or personal disaster would delay the release of either the PDF or eBook versions. Printing, shipping, and other fulfillment issues are not 100% in my control...but given the funds would be available in June...I do not foresee issues with getting the completed book to press in a timely enough manner so as to have it to your awaiting hands before Christmas of 2015.

I am totally committed to making this all happen, and am privileged to receive your support. Remember, I too was raised with blueprints---so, to our success!! ;-)

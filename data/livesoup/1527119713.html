About this project

THE STORY

Good evening, are you sitting comfortably? 

The basic premise behind these prints is that they are runts, outcast and forgotten about I've never seemed to get them to print. Some I underrated and so ignored. Others I doubted whether I'd got it 100% how I'd like and so squirrelled them away. Abused, forgotten, mistaken, betrayed and mistimed all these prints have gotten to the cusp of production and then fallen short. And so now sometimes years after they're production I've decided to force the issue and give these miscreants a chance at life, a crap series of Frankenstein's monsters if you will. Each print has a little story which I'll include under each image below. Enjoy this police lineup and see it as it can only be... A mess.

THE PRINTS

Is over 5 years old and was one of my first endeavours into street art and screen printing. It just seemed at the time that revolutionary Yorkshire gardener Alan Titchmarsh just wasn't that 'street', plus lacking confidence it could stand up against other graffiti artists in the lost file it went.

The veg patch edition below is a variant I made especially for kickstarter. The only thing red Alan Titchmarsh would support is his tomatoes come springtime.

Everything about this piece gives me the creeps which is weird as I produced it. The crosses on the eyes are some kind of malevolent taunt to the boatman on the river Styx. He's looking at you but he's not, the crosses obscure his eyes but you don't want to meet his stare. When I made this I think I thought that the implied reference to child abuse and the church were a bit too strong and I couldn't stomach it. In retrospect it's a burning effigy and one that can now haunt you. Don't like looking at it for too long though...

Continuing on the murderous child theme are three different versions of the piece 'Baby KILLA'. The colours are of the infant palate named after things loving parents would be interested in 'mothercare' and 'custard'. This saw a lot of action on the streets as it was a paste up poster I personally used all over the place but was also a freebie I gave out to people. I genuinely can't remember making this piece, all I remember was that it would be too awkward to produce just a few so I'd have to make a few different versions to make it possible. Thereafter it stalled. Originally I wanted the blocks to read 'Killer' as if proper spelling matters to a 2 year old murderer. Instead I went for the Wu Tang version 'KILLA' thus the child goes from a deranged serial killer in the making to a 90s gang banging infant.

For this Kickstarter venture I resurrected an old 'drips' style I used to use where I'd splurge paint on a wall then paste over it. You can the variant style screen print. The baked beans variant is just cause I really wanted a print with baked beans in it. Seemed appropriate.

 The idea with the 'Ravans' came to me whilst watching Ravens raiding rubbish bins next to McDonalds. Beside the pillaging were a group of Womies (white homies devoid of the slightest trace of gangsta) wearing cheap knock off Ray bans. I couldn't see the difference between the two groups. If anything the ravens would have stolen the glasses off the faces of the wude boys (white rude boys; it works in patterns) and were thus more credible than Wanilla Ice and the boys (Vanilla Ice but white...wait...). I blasted out the design for these guys quickly but then lost faith in myself as the imagery started to look more and more like an actual Raybans advert. As I pondered and pondered about what to do, my mind quickly drifted off into thinking up cheap ridicules of the white middle class...

 Originally I was going to stick this image over British 'give way' signs and a subversion of the road signs original meaning. Instead of give way to oncoming traffic, it was a distant plea and silent plea hidden in plain view as an ominous prediction of future doom.Not too bad an idea. Problem was I was too ambitious. I wanted this stuck up on a motorway give way sign pasted over it. And I managed it. But within two hours it was taken down. I tried again. Again down within a few hours. I tried a few more times but lost interest. The spot was prize but if no one saw it was it worth it? Maybe I could put them up on less policed but thus less exposed give way signs? But how could I forsake the highly viewed motorway for boring suburbia? Nobody tootalling along at 30mph in a 20mph zone gives a monkeys about the crumbling of mankinds future, let alone looks at road signs. Overconfidence vs incredulity. Neither won. The piece never saw light again.

 This 8 bit skull design was my fourier into retro 80s style icons. I used to DJ and this skull head was part of my logo. I never made it as a DJ (or an artist) and so I forgot about it.

Skullface was of course never made/born in the 80s and neither is the 90s variant. I wanted it to be as close to a shell suit as possible.

 Nazis and North Korea. Easy pickings for totalitarian mockery. The nazi slag fest is done. They're such a simple target my refined tastes (ahem) wouldn't allow such an easy satire. So Kim Jong UN it is then. The problem is somewhere down the line I lost my way and ended up mixing Ziggy Stardust with the Korean fat boy. But that wasn't my original purpose, I can't mix something infinitely cool with the infinite stomach of communisms poster boy. But I did and so I lost confidence (again) with my own ideas. I think the finished piece is good but as I lost the sense of what I made Ziggy Jong UN for I never published it. So here it is for you. Kim Jong Un bathed in the eternal glory of the North Korean flag.

The handfinished version I'm making for kickstarter is my attempt to get back to the fortress of juxtaposition. The gay pride flag painted over his face. Aren't I clever.

R

Risks and challenges

The single biggest risk to this project is ensuring the highest standards when it comes to print quality and speed of delivery when the project is funded. To help overcome this obstacle I have a host of excellent printers who can pick up the slack if anything should go wrong during the production of these screen prints. Also all postage will be insured will be sent express therefore minimising potentials losses in transit.

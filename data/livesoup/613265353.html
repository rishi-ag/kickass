About this project

Habitat is a whole new breed of sit/stand desk.

Habitat combines the design of fine furniture with the health and productivity benefits of an adjustable standing desk. Its unique design and mobility promote a collaborative work environment. We don’t just want to replace the desk—we want to kill the cubicle.

We found a lot of great standing desks out there on the market, but none that did quite what we wanted. We needed a simple-to-use, human-powered standing desk that could easily and quickly move between rooms and configurations. We wanted both personal space and ease of collaboration. We wanted beauty and sustainability. Since that perfect desk didn't seem to be out there, we started from scratch and made our own: a hybrid of fine furniture and practical work space.

Promote Collaboration and Raise Productivity

Habitat is an adjustable sit/stand desk that’s human-powered, mobile, and sustainably made. We designed Habitat from the ground up to be a modern-world alternative to the staid, cubicle-dominated office environment of old.

The need for this desk grew out of a small technology company where developers wanted to be able to quickly and easily shift between sitting and standing, and move their desks around in an open-plan office. Habitat lets them do that. We found that having the flexibility to sit, stand and move comfortably has significantly increased productivity and job satisfaction among our developers.

Adaptable, Agile and Easy to Adjust

Habitat has the largest adjustable range of any sit/stand desk that we’re aware of. We designed it to be comfortably used by people up to 6’8”, and it goes even higher than that! Our human-powered, motorless lift mechanism uses hydraulic pistons, which means the desk can be adjusted by hand and without power.

What’s more, it’s fast—it takes about 10 seconds to adjust Habitat between its lowest and highest positions. The desk surface adjustable range is 24” to 50” (attached monitor surface height range is 32” to 58”).

Death to the Cubicle

The traditional office layout is stagnant, isolating, unhealthy and unpleasant. We believe office spaces should promote collaboration by design, but allow for personal space and some measure of solitude when desired. All of these considerations express themselves in the design of Habitat. To us, Habitat represents more than a desk: it means the death of the cubicle.

Sustainable and Made in the USA

We wanted our desk to be made of high quality, sustainable materials. Unlike furniture made from particle board or molded plastic and metal, Habitat is made almost entirely of real wood. We use a premium, layered hardwood product composed of solid grade birch and maple wood from sustainably-managed forests. These all-hardwood-core panels are strong and lightweight, and they also machine beautifully, making them ideal for fine quality furniture. The fact that they’re manufactured right here in the Pacific Northwest is an extra bonus. We’re committed to our community. If our funding goal is reached, we’ll be able to expand into retail production at scale, creating jobs in our hometown of Eugene, Oregon.

With Habitat, we wanted to make a desk people could feel good about owning. A desk you can stand behind!

Who We Are

Player 1 Furniture is a collaboration of tech geeks and furniture designers determined to transform the office environment into a healthier, more productive space that invites teamwork and creative development. We grew out of the needs of our sister company, Lunar Logic, a small software firm where our developers are committed to the kind of collaborative work that generates the best productivity and end product. They needed sit/stand desks that allowed for quiet personal work as well as teamwork, that gave them mobility and flexibility in how and where they work.

The dream was only realized by bringing a team of experts together: a world class designer, people who have backgrounds in fine furniture construction, and tech developers who gave us invaluable feedback about functionality and ease of use. You could say we’re tech geeks by day and furniture designers by night.

How Funding Will Be Used

So far we've funded each prototype ourselves. Successful funding through Kickstarter will pay for materials and labor to manufacture the desk at scale. Specifically, we can purchase materials in bulk, greatly reducing the manufacturing cost of the desk and allowing us to offer it for sale to the public. We’ve partnered with a local woodworking and manufacturing company, and funding will also create new manufacturing jobs here in Eugene, OR.

Project Timeline

Risks and challenges

We’ve already ironed out many major issues through our prototyping process, however we may find that production does not scale according to our predictions, there may be unforeseen supply issues beyond our control, and if the project is more successful than we anticipate, we may find it difficult to meet a large volume of orders within the estimated delivery date. With our solid team background in design and manufacturing, we’re confident we can overcome these hurdles should they arise.

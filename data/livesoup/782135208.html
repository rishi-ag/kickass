About this project

 ● ● ●

✏︎ HELLO 

We want to create a unique, dual-purpose shop and studio. A space to view and buy affordable illustration that simultaneously offers the public a view into the creative process behind it. We want to grow Studio Mama Wolf by inspiring YOU!

✏︎ Our vision for Studio Mama Wolf is to create a beautiful place to meet and let people into our profession as illustrators.

Most artists & illustrators create behind closed doors. We want to throw ours wide open to the public with a dual-purpose shop and studio that allows a view of the creative process outside the once yearly ‘open studio’.

We want to cut out the middleman and offer special, handmade and crafted products directly from the creator. Studio Mama Wolf will not just exist as a place to sell but as a hub for advice, and for people to get involved. A space where you can view art and witness its creation first hand.

In addition to the shop we plan to introduce a special desk that will be offered to an emerging artist for a limited time. This will be a fantastic opportunity for new illustrators and designers to experience a busy creative studio and to try out their own goods in our shop.

Mama Wolf Studio wants to inspire and be inspired. We feel that growth happens through communication and interaction. We want to offer workshops, portfolio sessions, book signings and collaborate with the local arts community.We have so much to learn from each other and want to share this knowledge with you.

● ● ●

✏︎ What we will offer ...

  • A ‘hot desk’ for new and emerging illustrators to work alongside us and test out their products in our small shop.
  • Workshops
  • Portfolio feedback sessions
  • Advice to new artists
  • A space for book signings and launches
  • A space to hold monthly networking events
  • A place to connect
  • A permanent exhibition space
  • Collaboration with the local arts community

● ● ●

✏︎ Who we are ...

Studio Mama Wolf is an illustration studio housing three permanent creatives: Laura Hughes, Jill Tytherleigh, & Sandra Dieckmann. Our studio name grew naturally out of a client and friend calling Sandra 'Mama Wolf' and from there it took on a life of its own. We have been sharing a studio together since 2013. 

Laura works mainly on Children’s picture books and art for licensing. Her clients include Harper Collins U.S, Faber and Faber, American Greetings and Waitrose. She also enjoys curating exhibitions most recently the ‘100 Cats’ show featuring 100 pieces of feline-themed artwork. 

  • http://laurahughes-illustrator.co.uk 

Jill as well as working in publishing for Macmillan and Simon & Schuster has applied her work to a variety of projects like The Floating Cinema, transforming shop windows in Wilseden Green and Mitcham, food illustrations for Delicious Magazine & Daylesford Organic and packaging for big brands such as Ted Baker & Harrods. Jill enjoys exploring the medium of print having completed a course at the East London Printmakers.

  • http://jilltytherleigh.com

 Sandra has enjoyed running a successful Etsy shop online for years selling her often self initiated artwork which is also stocked in many places worldwide. Sandra's illustrations have also been applied to a wide array of international commissions and projects, working with client like Sky, Lily Cole, WWF, Andersen Press & Al Jazeera on book illustrations, editorial, apparel and much more. In 2013 Etsy flew her to Australia and New Zealand to speak at Semi Permanent Conferences about her life and work.

  • http://www.sandradieckmann.com

All three of us have been working as artists & illustrators for many years. We work within different specialties and between us we have worked for a variety of well-known UK based and international clients.

● ● ●

✏︎ OUR HOME

As well as making our work available in the Studio Mama Wolf shop, we want to be part of the blossoming creative community at Hackney Downs Studios where currently around forty new studios are being built. The area will soon include a theatre, print studio, offices and artists spaces, hosting brave entrepreneurs all carving out new, exciting and alternative ways in the world.

The shop will be located on the ‘Village Green’ at Hackney Downs Studios. The Village Green is open every day and currently houses a bike shop, a florist, a hairdresser, a wellness centre, art gallery, gym, cafe and soon, hopefully us!

Hackney Downs will once again open its doors on the 04.06.15 for a grand 'Open Day' which saw a huge crowd attend last year. We want to be finished by then to welcome you, and have a party and a drink to say thank you.

● ● ●

✏︎ The Move

The move will bring an array of new opportunities. All three of us are excited at the prospect of welcoming a wider audience into our studio practice.

We haven't encountered an illustration studio with open doors before and believe we are offering something truly unique. We want to invite you to pop in, have a coffee and a chat, and maybe buy a card or gift on your way past.

● ● ●

✏︎ WHERE DO THE FUNDS GO?

During this 30 day Kickstarter campaign we invite you to have a look at the special rewards we have on offer in return for your donation. We are aiming to cover the very basic expense of building and planning costs and are grateful for any help from you.

We have found a lovely space but in order to facilitate a dual-purpose shop and studio, some changes need to be made to incorporate a mezzanine level and stairs. For this reason we will need to pay for the following: 

  • Planning permission 
  • Structural engineer
  • Custom built stairs 
  • Materials 
  • Labour

We will also need money for the following: 

  • Shop fixtures and fittings 
  • Opening event
  • Rewards & postage

Any additional money we make over our target will be put towards making the studio a truly special place to come and visit.

We are hoping to start the build and refurbishments in the new studio on the 12th of May 2015. During that we will update you with photos and thoughts and whatever else is going on.

✏︎ WHAT WILL YOU GET IN RETURN ?

  • The reward of your choice
  • An invite to the opening party
  • The chance to be part of an exciting new creative venture 
  • Our eternal love and gratitude

Risks and challenges

We have signed the lease and are running against the clock to raise the funds to get this venture off the ground.

When we decided to open Mama Wolf to the public we never anticipated the costs involved. Studio space in London is very expensive and we only have a small area in which to accommodate both a shop and a working studio, hence the need for a mezzanine level.

We need any help we can get to raise the funds for an extra floor and staircase, as well as the fees for planning, building costs and insurance, plus additional extras like stock and rent overlap.

Kickstarter operates an all-or-nothing system, which means that if we don't achieve our target we will get nothing. Any small donation therefore is massively appreciated!

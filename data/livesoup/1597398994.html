An(other) unexpected zombie outbreak has brought the city to its knees. Society has collapsed to a nightmare state, and only the fittest or the luckiest have a chance of surviving this ordeal. Our protagonists fall on the latter camp. This group of fashionistas, together by trend rather than friendship, survived the apocalypse thanks to watching a four-hour unknown Austrian film with no subtitles at an arthouse cinema. Even zombies have standards! Luck will have them join a group of hardened survivors, who will see their resilience put to the test thanks to our main characters’ utter dumbness.

Hipsters vs. Zombies is a side-scrolling shoot’em-up-2D- Run & Gun. And, no, it’s no fluke. We were looking for the perfect match between our idea and what is well considered nowadays as, you know, vintage stuff. And so we did. Our main purpose has always been to provide the players with a real fun experience. To get this, we took reference from games such as Guacamelee, Metal Slug, Contra, Super Time Force Ultra, Cybernator or Gunstar Heroes. Classic (and not so classic) arcade games have always been an inspiration to us, because we grew up with them throughout the nineties, just during their fall and the consequent rise of video game consoles.

  •  Fight zombie hordes. Use custom hipster weaponry to destroy everything you find on the way. You will be provided with three different weapons for each character and a Warbucks Coffee, as decaffeinated as hot, really useful to melt the walking dead. 
  • Explore different levels full of joy and adventures. Blaze through 11 levels divided in two unique worlds where you will find plenty of nonsense jokes. Get the highest score to become the king (or queen) of Hipsterdom, even if you think you already are.
  • Recover lives as in old arcade games. Use your score to get new lives in order to save the hips… uhm, sorry, mankind. 
  • Discover the story behind the main characters. You already know their names and motivations; now, it’s time to find out how they hack their way through the Apocalypse and how far they can go... For a trendy cause. 
  • Face the incredible (though mainstream) final bosses! Hipsters vs. Zombies’ universe is full of weird and confusing situations. You will want to know what kind of sick, twisted mind created the final bosses you will have to fight.

- Ulysses

Ulysses, the most intense, laconic, and overacted lead character from “Hipsters vs. Zombies” He’s a conceited poet who boasts about being smarter than everyone else. He enjoys combining the latest high-tech gadgets with antique and vintage items. You can tell him out by his thick beard, fedora, and plaid shirt. A DJ record bag, a briar pipe, and the typewriter he always carries, because it’s no secret that:

“The pen is mightier than the sword. The typewriter is also heavier.”

- Sugar

Sugar, the “Hipsters vs. Zombies” Riot Grrrl! She’s a tough, independent girl, a regular at music festivals such as FIB, Coachella or Glastonbury. Despite being an indie girl, Sugar’s really concerned about her appearance. That’s why she spends 3 plus hours dressing herself up to get that well-studied careless urban outfit. You’ll recognize her by her long, pink and blue ombré, her shorts and Dr. Martens boots. All these always topped with her Wayfarer sunglasses and the proper accessories to rock the perfect look, for party, killin’ zombies, or whatevs! But hey, pals! Don’t let her looks deceive you, and don’t get on her bad side, either: When she gets a weapon, she’s a killing machine.

- The Zombies

We don’t really know how they turned into zombies. But we know how to take them out for sure. As you could imagine, zombies will be your greatest enemies. Unlike hipsters, the undead will not be provided with any specific weapon; however, they will throw objects, their own limbs, or even puke on us in order to wipe out the human race.

- Final Bosses

Final bosses have always been one of the funniest things in a shoot’em up game, so we didn't want to fall behind. Ours will be as funny as ridiculous and confusing.

Our characters had to accommodate to the new reality in order to fight the plague. But they did it their way… Their weaponry is introduced below:

Ulysses is a huge fan of vintage stuff, so he had to arm himself with the first (old) thing at hand.

  • CODE NAME: "180"  

180-gram of cool rhythm hurled from a vintage record bag are enough to slaughter zombie hordes.

  • CODE NAME: "SMG (Sub Musicassette Gun)"  

Two modified walkman as Uzis to shoot dozens of outdated cassettes per minute.

  • CODE NAME: "Full HD (Full Heavy Discthrower)"  

Ulysses also modified a Jukebox to be used as a burning record- shooting flame-thrower.

  • CODE NAME: "WTF (Weaponised Typing Furnishing)"  

His favorite typewriter has suffered more from his texts than from crushing zombies. Moreover, he has put a handle on it, and he uses it to beat the walking dead.

Sugar never settled for anything, and she has learnt to take advantage of everything around her in order to create genuine hipster weaponry.

  • CODE NAME: "The Dixon"

Crossbows are so mainstream… but combine one with the latest tattoo gun and add some color to the mix… Voilà! We got a real Weapon of Zombie Destruction.

  • CODE NAME: "Chicago Tattoowriter"  

Her grandpa used a Chicago Typewriter, and not exactly to write poetry. It has now been modified to drill zombies.

  • CODE NAME: "E.R. Special (Ellen Ripley Special)"

Sugar has always been a huge fan of Alien movies. She drew inspiration from Ellen Ripley’s gun to create a super tattoo gun fitted to pour vast amounts of flaming ink over the undead. Guaranteed destruction!

  • CODE NAME: "KuteKut"

Her father was a lover of cricket and her mother was a butcher; and she adores kittens. That’s how we get a cute lethal weapon. Pretty easy, right?

And of course, an item any Hipster would love to throw at the undead. The award- winning, famous, incredibly hot, practically decaf, all- original Warbucks napalmcino!!

Music and Words are two of the most important and necessary parts in any video game. In our desire to create a unique experience for future Hipsters vs. Zombies players, we want to introduce the four great professionals who are collaborating on our project. Referencing artists like Kavinsky, Birdy Nam Nam or Busy P., AVERNO and David Adkinson will be responsible for Hipsters vs. Zombies’ Original Soundtrack. Regarding the story, Adrián Benatar will help us understand the characters and what the Hipsters vs. Zombies universe is like.

We're a Spain-based duo of DJs and Producers with a peculiar style, a mix  of Techno, Electro and House. As fans of the audiovisual industry, we had created music inspired by cinema before, but never imagined we would have the chance to produce music for a videogame, blending past, present and future with our dark, electronic style. We’re so glad to take part in this project.

I’m a half-Spanish half-North American musician, producer, and composer of original soundtracks who has been surrounded by music his whole life. I began studying piano at the age of 3, and composing at 12, respectively. I have an eclectic taste, and it is reflected on my music, minimalistic and obscure, with influences from both, classical music, such as Romanticism and Impressionism, and modern and electronic music. To me, audiovisual resources are the best way to try out different styles, and doing it for a videogame project like HvsZ is a unique experience.

BIO: "Adrián is almost 2 meters tall and has a lumberjack beard. He began to write at a very early age, and, since then, he has been challenging himself in different areas: plays, comics and shortcuts scripts, storytelling, poetry, and, now, video games, too. He has also carried out several projects such as leading creative writing and screenwriting workshops, cultural magazine –Oveja negra- and a particular blog –Relatos ilustrados. In general he’s a nice guy, a good conversationalist with a serious look on his face, but sounds so wacky when he refers to himself in third person."

I’ve always been a creative person and I stand out for being open to new and interesting ideas. I have spent most of my life in graphic design and subsequently in 3D design, too. Video Games have always been a big part of my life; that’s why I work as a game developer nowadays. 

I’ve been playing videogames for almost as long as drawing, becoming good at only one of both. I have done several animation shorts, and also worked as an illustrator for music magazines, movie posters, horror novels and coaching books (Yep. I was young and I needed the money). In the last few years, I’ve been focusing on videogames, and my inner nerd couldn’t be happier. I also have a webcomic (Odiolitos), usually drawn when I should be sleeping. 

Travel is my passion. I always try to create the moment and foster the circumstances in order to pack my bags and go back on the road. Most often, though, I don’t need to take a trip or even leave my home, because every project I begin is like a personal and emotional journey, full of challenges and ambitions. No matter how many I‘ve made, or even all the years dedicated to animation production and, now, video games… Everytime I start something new, I feel the same thrill I got the first time. And here I am, on the road… Again! 

After a lot of years and money spent on arcade games, here I am, involved in one of the most absurd, bizarre and amusing projects I ever imagined. #HvsZ is dedicated to all of those crazy ideas rejected because of a bad Marketing Planning. P.S.: I promise to include a Dinosaur-Robot-Giant-Zombie as a Final Boss if the Kickstarter finally succeeds. I swear. 

I’ve always been glued to a screen, putting every video game polygon under the microscope. Ever since the Nintendo 64 was released, I knew my fate was to create something similar to those things I saw in my old 13”tv. I work as a video game programmer and I can’t avoid checking brand new techniques. Sometimes I need someone to rip the keyboard out of my hands to get rest.

Hipsters vs. Zombies is a high-risk project, not only for having two of the most current and hackneyed cultural issues as its leitmotiv, but also for the choice of a specific video game subgenre. Though the project is underway, we need to know if we could count on gamers community support. There is still a long way to go to become Hipsters vs. Zombies in what we really want, because it is a primitive product that needs to progress in the right direction. That is the reason why we believe a Kickstarter campaign is the best channel to know potential gamers interests and also, thanks to the community, to bring the final product the required added value. The more financial support we get, the more freedom we will have in developing the videogame. For instance, we could hire new professionals in order to improve the expected result of the project.

Risks and challenges

A huge investment of time and effort is required to develop any game. The major risk this project has to face is failing to meet launch deadlines, despite having studied all possible situations and being aware of any unexpected event which can lead to a delay in the launch date. Even so, the whole Hipsters vs. Zombies team is working hard on developing the game. We trust the gamer community so much that we resort to them getting involved in funding the project. Relying on your support during the videogame development empowers us and is a guaranteed success to carry this project out. We are fully committed to you all and your final enjoyment, so we pledge to make regular progress updates.

Of course, we are always at your disposal for any further information you may need about the project.

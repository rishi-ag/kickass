About this project

Above is a series trailer made from existing footage, as well as some previously never seen before footage. All of this will be reshot in the new series.

Do you like fake mustaches? Real mustaches? Explosions? Ninjas? Muscle cars? Girls with eyepatches? Conspiracies? Synth music? Good puns? ... Bad puns...? Check out the original student film below!

A Little About Ace's History

Four long years ago, in 2011, I moved to Cheney, WA to attend Eastern Washington University's (EWU) film program. There, I met Gus (Goose) Froese. We bonded over our love for old action movies, namely Die Hard and Lethal Weapon, and our senses of humor. One day, we stumbled across an Internet gem and it changed our lives forever. If you haven't seen "Hard Streets," I suggest watching it now. After seeing it, we set out to make our own police parody- only... Better.Â 

Thus, Ace Dangerman was born. It took us years, but we finally perfected a story, characters, and overall world that suited our idea best, and set to work.Â 

With a $1 budget (for a dollar store, stick-onÂ mustache), we made the first Ace Dangerman for our production class. With a run-time of about 17 minutes, "Ace Dangerman: Loose-Cannon Law-Man (LCLM)" only took around 10 hours to film. The film quality is admittedly not the greatest, but hey, what can you expect on a budget of literally $1 and a 10 hour shoot time? To our surprise, it was extremely well received, by teachers and the public alike, and Gus, who plays Ace, became practically a local hero.

We even received praise from some of the actors and creators of the popular sci-fi parody web series, "Transolar Galactica!" They attended EWU's film program too!

Gus and I were confident we wouldn't let Dangerman die, and thus, we ended LCLM on a cliffhanger. We were determined to make it bigger.

When the next quarter rolled around, we went big. And failed. We intended to make the sequel, "Ace Dangerman: Vendetta of Revenge (VoR)," however, half way through pre-production, we realized our grand idea, our pride and joy, was much too large scale for a zero budget production.

Oops.

Ace Dangerman... Will Return?

But like Jack Bauer, Ace Dangerman can live another day. But this time, he needs your help.

We're setting out to make Ace Dangerman a phenomenon, by remaking the first short film into one season of 10 or so short format web series episodes, ranging between 5 and 8 minutes. When watched one after another, the first season will form a complete short film, ranging between 60 and 90 minutes. These episodes will retell the original story, as well as expand on it. Visually-wise, there willÂ a slightly darker tone, and a bluer, more 80s look, rather than the 70s yellow from the original. Photos marked with (New look) are an example of what new material will look like.

We will be filming in and around Seattle, which will act as our Golden City. using all brand new equipment, purchased by myself for my freelance video production business, Faulty Wire Studios. Bad audio plagued the first film, we have new mics. We barely had any lights. Now I have five. Camera work was handheld and not 80s enough in general. We'll be using more tripod and stabilizers. Ace will have a car, (he apparently walks everywhere in the original) a black 1980s Corvette that we will be renting from a friend. We plan to spend more than 10 hours shooting this time around. We have so much more experience and resources than we had a year ago, the only thing stopping us is that the budget is slightly out of our reach.

This campaign is to fund only the pilot episode of the remake of "Ace Dangerman: Loose-Cannon Law-Man," to see if you're interested in seeing this and more in the future.Â If successfully funded and well received, we intend to campaign for funds for the rest of the season, and after that until we run out of stories, or the world has had enough of Ace.

Gus is quoted as saying, "If I can go to Comic-con and see someone dressed as Ace, then I can die happy." Let's not let Gus down!

Season One - Plot

Ace Dangerman (Gus Froese) and Booker (Aaron Taylor) are detectives for the Golden City Police Department (GCPD), lead by The Chief (Kyle Beckwith). But when Booker is killed in the line of duty, Ace goes off the deep end.

He is dismissed from the police force, but when a new lead on Booker's killer is found, it's up to Ace to learn more. Fueled by revenge, Ace will stop at nothing until Booker's killer is brought to justice. Until, that is, evidence of deep corruption within GCPD is uncovered. That, andÂ Booker may still be alive. Action, mystery, comedy; all delivered in one magnificent double homage/parody to everything you love about 80s movies.

In the original film, Booker only gets about a minute of screen time. Aaron is a great actor and we've always regretted having to cut his time short. The pilot episode we will be filming is an extended version of the first minute of the film, exploring the backgrounds of Ace and Booker's relationship, as well as set the plot, tone and style of the entire series.

Intended to be four seasons, consisting of Loose-Cannon Law-Man, Vendetta of Revenge, Maximum Overkill, and Terminal Fatality, we have no shortage of material, and we'd love to share it all with you! This pilot is just the beginning for Ace!

Where's My Money Going?

We're only asking for $1,000! Seeing as the first film was shot for $1, our first episode should be at least 1,000 times better than our original film! Imagine the possiblities an entire season could present!Â 

A good portion of it is going right back to you, our loyal and newfound fans, in the form of awesome t-shirts, posters and other fun stuff!

All of our actors are extremely dedicated and work for free, and I direct, edit, do visual effects, basically every portion of the post-production process in my free time.

Since filming the first episode, I've spent literally every penny I've made on film equipment. Better mics, better lenses, shoulder mounts, dollies, etc. Everything equipment-wise wrong with the first film has been fixed, so absolutely no money will go to equipment.

Unfortunately, not everyone is willing to play along for free.

Filming in small town Cheney, we were able to film on public streets and in school buildings, absolutely free. But not this time around. Filming in Seattle and the surrounding areas, a good portion of this budget will go to securing locations (a diner and an industrial building) for as cheap as we can find.

The remaining budget will be used for transporting actors, as well as improving our costumes/props and renting a 1980 Corvette from a friend's dad for Ace and Booker to cruise in.

Any money over our $1,000 goal will go directly into producing the following episodes of the season.

All we want is another opportunity to share Ace with the world and deliver quality content that people can enjoy, and we'd love for you to be a part of that.

Risks and challenges

Our biggest challenge will be catapulting Ace Dangerman out of obscurity, during this funding stage and once it's filmed and released, gaining enough traction that people want us to keep making them! But we're confident in our character, world and our abilities as storytellers that this will be a success. We draw inspiration from Die Hard, Lethal Weapon, countless Shwartzenegger films, Magnum PI, Knight Rider and more, and our hope is that anyone who loves these films as much as we do will love Ace Dangerman as well.

Gus and I, along with our dedicated cast and crew, are extremely passionate about filmmaking and our original idea and will do anything necessary to keep it alive and deliver exactly what we promised you; the highest quality pilot as humanly possible.

About this project

LoveLive! School Idol Festival is a rhythm game developed by KLab. You can download it on iOS and Android. It is based on the LoveLive! School Idol Project franchise. The majority of players is in Japan (more than 9 millions players) but a big community also exists worldwide. See Official Site for more information.

School Idol Tomodachi is a website that gathers and provides resources for players of the mobile game LoveLive! School Idol Festival.

  • Get all pictures, information & statistics about the cards in the game!
  • Get trivia & information about all the idols in the game!
  • Create a profile and enter your cards to show them to your friends!
  • Follow what your friends are doing on the game and comment on their profiles!
  • Get information and a countdown for all the events in Japanese & English version!
  • Vote for your favorite idol & the prettiest cards on the global contest and on special contests!
  • And much more: check out the list of features!

 It started 3 months ago, and today reached over 6000 users!

We've been developing it in our spare time, in addition to our full time jobs. We pay for the servers ourselves, and it's getting a bit expensive; due to the necessity of high performance hardware and good reliability. However, we really want to keep it free to use and avoid displaying annoying advertisements. That's why today, we need help from the community to help us cover the server costs!

We hope that you understand and that you will help us keep going!

If this campaign is successful, we plan to add two major features:

Songs!

Get information, search/filter and track your scores/trophies on songs! You loved it on the cards, we're going to do it with the songs as well!

You'll be able to search and filter songs using titles, sub-units, attribute, order songs by notes, length, LPs required, and more!

You'll also have a Songs section in your profile where you'll be able to track your scores and trophies. You'll even be able to say whether the song was played in a Score Match, a Medley or as a regular live stage.

 Teams!

Sort your cards by teams or ask the website to automatically generate optimized teams for you!

You'll be able to show your teams on your profile! Each team will have a name and 9 cards. You'll be able to add as many teams as you want!

We will also provide a tool that will automatically create teams for you, depending on the attribute, and your needs:

  • If you want to get a high score, we will calculate the statistics & bonds
  • If you want to get a full combo, we will fill your team with Perfect Lock cards
  • If you want to survive the hardest songs, we will fill your team with Healer cards

And more!

  • We will redesign the profile page to make it prettier
  • We will redesign the homepage to show relevant information to you
  • We will improve the activities to make them more interesting: "Like" activities, post your own activities with free text and pictures, filter activities (only SR/UR), etc.
  • We will improve your experience on mobile
  • We will make the transfer code storage more secure
  • We will redesign the idols pages to improve navigation and make them prettier
  • We will add cool new things to the cards: version name, tags, transparent images, see who else own the card, and more
  • And lots of other features. You won't be disappointed ;)
We have so much more to add and we have new ideas of features everyday. If we exceed the goal, we will cry, then dance, then we will make sure School Idol Tomodachi becomes incredibly awesome and fun!

The box

Besides the rewards fees, we will entirely use it to pay for the server. The server costs approximatively $75 / month.

It sounds expensive, but understand that we have a lot of traffic, which requires us to pay for a very good server and lots of bandwidth.

Transparency & honesty are our priorities! That's why we published a full detailed budget here. Feel free to comment or ask questions about it :)

During the first month of existence of the website, we had a pretty standard server that was inexpensive (AWS Free tier). Most of the time, people would get errors because the server couldn't handle all the requests, and we had to pay for a lot of extra bandwidth (first bill was $200). As a result, we had to migrate multiple times until we could find the best option.

Today we have a pretty robust architecture that corresponds to our needs!

  • The server runs on Amazon Web Services EC2
  • We have a t2 medium instance with 2 CPUs and 4 GiB RAM
  • We store the images (cards, events banners) on S3
We're currently migrating:
  • EC2 used to be in Tokyo, is now in North Virginia
  • S3 is in Tokyo, will migrate to North Virginia June 1st

Our project is open-source and open for contributions. We are more than happy to accept pull-requests on GitHub! To learn more about the source code, see our contributors guides.

We are two developers and relatively casual players of the game. It's the first project we made together.

We're both 23 years old and we live in San Francisco, California. We come from the same computer science university and both work as software engineers in the same company: http://life.tl/

We started coding School Idol Tomodachi in January 2015. The reason why we started it was because we used to update spreadsheets to track our progress, but we thought that we could probably find a better way to do it. So we made it.

At first it was just an API. We knew we were not the only ones who were tracking cards, so to make it easier to use, we made a website on top of the API. And to make the website more fun, we also made a contest.

At some point, we decided to talk to some IRC + Reddit folks, and a few tests and feedback gathering later, we launched School Idol Tomodachi to the public on February 2nd!

The LoveLive! community got interested in it and started asking for more features, which we happily added.

From a simple cards tracker, it became a full-featured website on its own that gathers the community around the game and keeps providing players nice resources to help them play the game.

This heartwarming support from the community keeps motivating us to do more! We love helping the community by providing as much information as we can about the game, while keeping the website easy to use, shiny, cute and pink ^_^

We don't know how far we will go with this, but one thing we know for sure is that we are having a lot of fun doing it and sharing this crazy idol adventure with you.

Risks and challenges

We also have a full time job, so our schedule cannot be guaranteed, since we work on this on our spare time. We're pretty confident though, since so far we met all our goals in time.

Generally speaking, running the website without the financial help of the community might be very hard, because we don't have extra money to spend on the website.   • Why can't you pay for it yourself?

    At first, we thought we could run it on a free server (aws free tier), but it didn't work (see Server & Tech Specs), so we had to think of a way to pay for it.

    We're currently balancing our lives between working for a startup that we founded and doing freelance missions to sustain ourselves financially. We have absolutely no extra money to spend on this website nor buying lovecas in the game ;)

    If we can't pay for it and nobody thinks it's worth paying for it, we'll regretfully close it, but that's all we can do. If there's a community behind it that wants it alive and accepts to help us by paying for it, we will keep maintaining it. We think it's a fair trade!

    Last updated: Sun, May 17 2015 12:18 am EDT
  • Are servers really that expensive?

    Yes, they are.

    And no, we can't just get a less performant one. We tried. We even spent multiple days trying to optimize every single database query to make the page load faster. We cache pages and assets. Still, smaller AWS instances couldn't handle all the requests during rush hours.

    We don't want to host the site on a dedicated server. Maintaining them is a pain, the bandwidth offered by cheap providers is not reliable, and you have to do everything yourself. Cloud hosting on the other hand, offers a large range of services that make server management a piece of cake, which suits our needs since this is just a side project for us, after all.

    Last updated: Sat, May 16 2015 11:47 pm EDT
  • Why is it hosted in Japan?

    When we launched the website, we thought Japanese people would be more interested, since there are way more players there than in the rest of the world, so the fist version was only available in Japanese. At some point we realized that it was very hard to communicate about our website if we couldn't speak the language, so we started targeting the English-speaking community.
    We're glad we did, because the community is absolutely lovely and much bigger than we thought!

    The server is still in Japan for this reason. However, since it costs a bit more than hosting it in other countries, we're currently discussing to eventually move it somewhere else. We will keep you updated about this. If it ends up costing less, it means your donations will help us for a longer time!

    Last updated: Sun, May 17 2015 12:18 am EDT
  • Why Kickstarter?

    Though some projects use Kickstarter to drive new potential users to their service, we mostly count on our existing community to back us up. The reason why we picked Kickstarter out of many other crowdfunding platforms is that it's well known and trusted, which will make our backers feel more comfortable donating.

    Some people also wondered why we didn't just add a "Donate" button on our website. Well, we actually have one, and actively communicated about it and our needs. So far we collected ~$35, which is very nice, but unfortunately it is not enough to cover the server costs.

    A crowdfunding platform, on the other hand, lets people be part of an adventure and get rewards for their donations. It's organized during a certain period, which makes it more exciting. It shares the whole story, it comes with new goals to improve the website and updates throughout the whole campaign.

    We also like that it's a one-time thing. Once we raise enough funds, we will not have to ask for money again in a very long time!

    We believe doing it this way is much more fun for both owners (us) and backers (our wonderful community)~

    Last updated: Mon, May 18 2015 10:24 am EDT

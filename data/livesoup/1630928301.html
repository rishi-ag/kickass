About this project

As seen on:

Read the Gravel Cyclist review here!

Last year, our company, Grava Bikes, successfully launched a full carbon "any road" disc-brake bike, dubbed the "Maple Sally", Maple Sally Road being one of our favorite dirt road ride routes. We love the properties of carbon and the Maple Sally has performed with perfect aplomb in road, gravel grind and cyclocross competitions across the country, but we started to think about what to do next.

We've been riders and racers since the '80s and the indelible images of Classics hard men fighting the elements and the cobblestones of northern Europe were the key in our formative years as young American cyclists. We started to dream up a steel frame that recalled the spirit of the classic era and combined it with the best of the modern era and beyond. Enter "The Revenuer".

Named after the law men of the defunct prohibition enforcement unit of the US Department of Revenue, the Revenuers once roamed our area rooting out the illegal moonshine production in the foothills of the Blue Ridge and Appalachian mountains. "The Revenuer" is a tough, lightweight, performance built steel frame for tackling anything you throw at it.Â 

We didn't want to make another "me too" frameset that jumped on the gravel grinding bandwagon, so we set out to make a frame that looked forward to emerging trends in cycling as a way to set ourselves apart.Â 

The Revenuer is one of the very few production steel frames to use front and rear thru axles to maximize stiffness and performance.

It features clearance for tires up to 44mm wide.

It has a 44mm head tube to utilize the now standard tapered steerer tubes found on modern disc brake carbon forks.Â 

It also has a PF30 bottom bracket shell to accommodate any crankset as well as allowing for easy set up as a single speed with the use of a PF30 compatible eccentric bottom bracket.

Â The frame is powder coated in Model T black for increased durability.

Built from a custom blend of Dedaccai and Columbus Zona tubing for maximum performance.

You can view more of the technical specifications at www.gravabike.com/bike-the-revenuer/

For our carbon frame, we turned to Asia to capitalize on the low cost production and high quality standards that manufacturers there have to offer. This is the same reason all of the big name bike companies look there as well. But this project allowed us to take advantage of the explosion of small frame building operations in the US and we were lucky enough to find one in Chattanooga, TN, not far fromÂ our neck of the woods. By producing this frame in the US with a single frame builder, we have maintained close involvement with every detail of the build process. Â 

The funds we seek to raise will fund our initial production run of frames. We have already had prototypes produced and are currently riding and racing them to make sure that everything is exactly the way we want it. The prototyping process has been a great experience as we work through all of the design elements with the builder, work with our graphic artist to make sure that we get the right look for all of our decals, and in general, work to produce the best frame we can. The difficult parts of this project are already behind us. All we need now is help with funding. Our builder is ready to go and his estimated time of delivery for the initial production run of 20 frames is 5 to 6 weeks. Once we meet this initial production number, we will not have to make such large reorders and this new steel model will be a sustainable part of our business.

Risks and challenges

Our chief challenge that we foresee is in the availability of TRP forks to go with the framesets. During our prototype production, frames were completed but availability of the forks caused us to have incomplete framesets. These forks are in higher demand than TRP anticipated and supply runs out quickly. We have established an Original Equipment Manufacturer account with them (as well as Cane Creek and DT Swiss) which gives us priority ahead of aftermarket wholesalers. They have been easy to work with and enthusiastic about our spec'ing their fork on this frameset.

Building the prototypes taught us a lot about the actual product and enabled us to make a lot of tweaks to the design and the equipment choices that would have slowed down fulfillment if we had not produced a frame prior to the Kickstarter funding. We have addressed a lot of the little issues that would have slowed production, including moving cable stops by a few millimeters, incorporating S-bend chainstays to facilitate greater heel and crankarm clearance, and increasing the size of the decals to eliminate gapping when they wrap around the tube.

During the prototype production run, our framebuilder got to work through a lot of the issues he might encounter when producing a large number of stock frames at once. He learned to anticipate production issues and to have backup materials on hand in order to eliminate delays. He also has the actual process of building our frames honed to the most efficient method.

While we know that some unforeseen issues may arise when we step up to the full production run, we have established clear lines of communication with all parties involved to make sure that we can address problems quickly and effectively with as little loss of time as possible.

We will communicate any production issue that may arise as soon as we encounter it and will give estimated timelines for completion and delivery if they change. Our experience owning a bike shop has taught us the importance of prompt delivery to the customer and how to work with vendors to ensure quickest delivery.

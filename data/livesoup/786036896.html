About this project

I started creating different lip balms in my kitchen because I was not pleased with what was available on the market.  I do not like thick lip balms, that feel like they are coating my lips.

After a year of testing and trial and error, I came up with a lip balm that is gluten and paraben free, made in Canada, with a great deal of moisturizing properties, never feels heavy, and comes in a variety of fragrances.  The ingredients are great for individuals with eczema, and more mature woman (since one of the ingredients is great at replenishing mature skin).   The product itself has been tested in Exuma, London, USA, Australia and Canada (summer and winter, great for all season!).  These test sites have given me the ability to test in every different environment I could possibly think of.

Scandalous Lip Balms are great for using as a base layer for your lipstick.  It does help lipstick stay on longer while moisturizing and protecting your lips.  It's a triple threat!

I have produced Scandalous Lip Balms is 22 different flavors/fragrances.  Which vary from Pina Colada, Banana, Peppermint, Cinnamon, to Watermelon and Strawberry Kiwi.  My favorite is Tutti Fruitti, however Chocolate and Creamsicle are a close second.

I find that men prefer the Creamsicle flavor as it reminds them of the orange Creamsicle Popsicle we used to buy at 7-11.

This has been a dream come true and the effort has been rewarded by sales from WagJag, and other sites.  However I am interested in re-branding the product and moving it into stores.  Therefore I need help with packaging, web sites, and standardizing the labels.

I hope you love my product as much as I do.

Risks and challenges

The risk and challenges I have with the product is re-branding it in a standardized way. I feel that the lip balms and their corky names such as Bahama Mama Pina Colada, Cool & Crazy Coconut, and Provocatively Perky Peppermint, are hard to standardize with the same font and photos. I find it difficult to find a supplier for the tins I like to use and silk screening of the tins. You need to order a standard amount for the best deals, and I simply do not have the funds to test every tin I would like. Packaging has become a challenge as everyone would like to make a test sample but that is expensive. There are so many choices when it comes to packaging, because I would like to sell them in packs of three, which could fit into a box, or a tin, or tubes, whether they are see through or solid. I have the product I need someone to help with packaging. I have tried several times on my own to standardize the product, with no avail. I believe I need to hire a company to help standardize the fonts/pictures, etc, as well as help me with the packaging. I would like to use Canadian companies as we have several great packaging companies that I believe could do a great job. Selling it hasn't been a problem, once people try it, they come back for more.

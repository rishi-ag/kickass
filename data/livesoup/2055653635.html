About this project

At Kurt’s we make high quality hemp t-shirts with beautiful and modern designs, that are durable and are produced locally by real craftsmen. With the combination of hemp and natural indigo, we give you a unique t-shirt from sustainable materials, printed with traditional techniques of silk screening or dyed in indigo.

Kurt’s believes that we should again follow our heart when making clothing. Something which the fashion industry of today has lost when fast fashion came to the stage. With that, a few beautiful and sustainable techniques and crafts have been forgotten. That’s what we want to change.

The tailor in every designer should step up again. Making clothing should be done with passion and love for the product, the design and the environment. Our dream is to make a product that is sustainable, has a unique design and that is made locally with real craftsmanship.

We took a deep dive into the old techniques, fibers and production processes and found the answer in hemp, natural indigo dying and silkscreen printing. Hemp is a long forgotten and truly sustainable fiber that is comfortable, strong and breathable. In our opinion it is the perfect material to make clothing. In silkscreen printing and natural indigo dying we came upon the workmanship we were looking for.

This combination makes a Kurt’s a unique t-shirt that has the quality and durability of the old days, with the look & feel of today.

We offer you three types of t-shirts: 

A plain Kurt’s keeps it simple – this t-shirt consists simply of three components: hemp, organic cotton and a tiny bit of lycra. Add a pocket to it and you have a stylish basic.

 An indigo dyed Kurt’s is dyed by hand in a big barrel, full of natural indigo. In this way the t-shirt will turn completely blue. The combination of hemp and organic cotton, gives a deep and rich color effect, like it has been worn for years.

A printed Kurt’s is like a painting on a t-shirt. We teamed up with the Dutch design house ‘Paapje’ and developed a few prints ourselves. These artworks are all silkscreen printed by hand, piece by piece. We use water base, low-impact textile ink for this process. 

You can choose from a collection of 13 styles, some in light blue others with a dark blue color. See below for all options.

*please note all t-shirts come with a chest pocket and a round cut bottom.

In our t-shirts, each component has its own function: 

  • The hemp makes it sustainable and environmentally friendly 
  • The organic cotton makes it more comfortable to wear
  • The lycra brings it back into shape after wearing and washing

A Kurt’s stands for a high quality and durable product, with a great deal of attention for its look & feel and for the way it’s being made. Our t-shirts can be recognized by its round cut bottom, it’s distinctive prints and our little friend the swallow, flying in the corner of your t-shirt.

We only work together with local partners and artisans who understand what real craftsmanship is about. In all we do, we have great respect for the environment and the people we work with. We are a forward thinking fashion brand that wants inspire others. We start with a t-shirt. We end up an entire collection.

 Hemp is strong and sustainable and has been used in many industries for centuries. Amsterdam sailors made their sails and ropes from hemp. Henry Ford once made a car from hemp. Word goes around that even the first Levi’s trousers were manufactured from hemp. For a long time, hemp was a forgotten resource. If you look at all its advantages, that’s something hard to understand.

As said above, it’s softer, stronger and more breathable than cotton. In addition, hemp is a natural fiber with an extremely low impact on the environment. At Kurt’s we share a love for natural indigo (indigofera tinctoria Linn), a central theme used in all of our products.

Unfortunately, natural dying and hand looming traditions are disappearing fast. We are here to keep this wonderful and generations old craftsmanship alive, by translating it into a modern and wearable product.

 Our t-shirts have a:

  • Regular fit
  • Slightly longer and round cut bottom
  • Slightly wider neckline
  • Small pocket on the chest 

 Sizes ranging from XS-XL

As you can see, the tees look great on both men and women. Please check the size chart to see which size suites you best.

When you order your T-shirt via our campaign, you can't select your size and print immediately. We’ll reach out to you once the campaign has ended to help you find the one that is right for you.

To get the best color effect on hemp made fabrics, silkscreen printing is the way to go. Moreover, silkscreen printing is the perfect technique for our all-over prints. And it’s the only way to print the artworks of Paapje on a t-shirt decently.

Next to that, silkscreen printing is an amazing craft that puts real workmanship in the production of our t-shirts.

Paapje
Paapje is our silkscreen print partner from a small town in the eastern part of the Netherlands. The designs of Paapje are painted or drawn directly on the silkscreens. This makes every meter unique. Paapje uses historic silkscreens with original designs that have been well preserved. Often, the print designs were inspired by nature. The Paapje prints are used for modern textile designs for which they work with sustainable fabrics like organic cotton, hemp, silk and wool. 
www.paapje.nl

Blueprint Amsterdam
Blue Print is our specialist for all our natural indigo dying processes, based in the center of Amsterdam. They know how to handle this blue gold and use exclusively natural methods whilst doing so.
www.blueprintamsterdam.com

Manufactured in Portugal
Our factory, where all our t-shirts are manufactured, is based in Porto, Portugal. We have a close relationship with them and know their skills in making high quality fabrics, as they are completely transparent about their ways of working.

With the manufacturing happening in Portugal and the indigo dying and silkscreen printing in the Netherlands, we keep our production process as local as possible. Eventually, we want to bring the entire production process of Kurt’s Amsterdam to the Netherlands. Unfortunately this is not yet possible, as we can’t fully guarantee the same high quality standards as we get from Portugal.

We expect to ship the first batch 10 weeks after the campaign is funded succesfully. This means that the first shipment will send out the second week of August.

Join us on this mission to bring back the quality and durability of the old days, with the look & feel of today.

For more information about Kurt’s Amsterdam, you can visit our website: www.kurts-amsterdam.com or like us on Facebook and follow us on Instagram.

Risks and challenges

All is set to launch Kurt’s Amsterdam successfully. The designs are ready. We have made some very clear understandings with our suppliers. All the logistics are well thought through.
And it’s not the first time we are manufacturing and selling clothing. We have a solid experience in this business, know which hiccups can occur and how to tackle them.

Our main challenge is making sure that the process of manufacturing goes smoothly without delays. We work together with the best in the industry and put great trust in them. But of course, something can always go wrong. Therefore, we will monitor this process closely to prevent this from happening.

Another challenge is that all of our printing is done by hand. Though, this gives character to the end result. Yet, if our campaign goes really well and we have a lot of backers, this could also be a reason why the production process can get delayed. So we will put our best efforts in making sure this process goes well.

Throughout the campaign we will communicate openly and keep you up to date at all times to ensure that you know what's going on.
